       26. Wealth Flow (First-Order ODE)
         - Formula: dW = α × W_source × (1 - resistance)

         - Returns: Wealth delta

       27. Class Dynamics Derivative (Four-Class System)
         - Formula (First-Order):
         dW₁/dt = α₄₁W₄ + α₃₁W₃ + α₂₁W₂ - δ₁W₁
       dW₂/dt = α₃₂W₃ + α₄₂W₄ - α₂₁W₂ - δ₂W₂
       dW₃/dt = α₄₃W₄ + γ₃ - α₃₁W₃ - α₃₂W₃ - δ₃W₃
       dW₄/dt = -(dW₁ + dW₂ + dW₃)  [constraint]
         - Four Classes:
             - W₁: Core Bourgeoisie (Top 1%)
           - W₂: Petty Bourgeoisie (90-99%)
           - W₃: Labor Aristocracy (50-90%)
           - W₄: Internal Proletariat (Bottom 50%)
         - Extraction Rates (fitted from FRED DFA 2015-2025):
             - α₄₁ (proletariat→bourgeoisie)
           - α₃₁ (labor aristocracy→bourgeoisie)
           - α₂₁ (petty bourgeoisie→bourgeoisie)
           - α₃₂ (labor aristocracy→petty bourgeoisie)
           - α₄₂ (proletariat→petty bourgeoisie)
           - α₄₃ (proletariat→labor aristocracy)
         - Redistribution Rates:
             - δ₁ (from bourgeoisie, progressive taxation)
           - δ₂ (from petty bourgeoisie)
           - δ₃ (from labor aristocracy)
         - Imperial Rent:
             - γ₃ (superwages injection to labor aristocracy)
         - Existing GameDefines: ClassDynamicsDefines with all α, δ, γ coefficients
       28. Wealth Acceleration (Second-Order Dynamics)
         - Formula: d²W/dt² = β(dW/dt) - ω²(W - W*)
         - Parameters:
             - wealth_share (W)
           - velocity (dW/dt)
           - equilibrium (W* attractor)
           - damping (β, negative = mean-reverting)
           - frequency (ω, natural frequency)
         - Returns: Second derivative
         - Theory: Models momentum and oscillation around equilibrium
         - Property: Empirically equilibrium from FRED DFA:
             - W₁* = 0.305 (30.5% for bourgeoisie)
           - W₂* = 0.382 (38.2% for petty bourgeoisie)
           - W₃* = 0.294 (29.4% for labor aristocracy)
           - W₄* = 0.020 (2% for proletariat)
       29. Full Dynamics (Combined First + Second Order)
         - Returns: (first_derivatives, second_derivatives)
         - Application: Complete wealth distribution evolution
         - Properties:
             - Sum-to-one invariant maintained
           - Momentum effects captured via second-order terms
           - Resistance factors dampen extraction rates
       30. Equilibrium Deviation
         - Formula: deviation = Σ(W_i - W_i*)²
         - Parameters: wealth_shares, equilibrium (optional, defaults to FRED values)

         - Constant: HOURS_PER_YEAR = 2080

       B. Imperial Rent Calculation (TVT Axioms E3-E4)

       Module: src/babylon/economics/melt/imperial_rent.py

       - ImperialRentCalculator Protocol
         - Formula 1 (Φ_hour): Φ = (W/τ) × (1/γ_basket) - 1
             - Measures extraction rate (flow), NOT class position
           - Positive: worker extracts labor through consumption
           - Negative: worker is net exploited
           - Range: approaches -1 as W→0
         - Formula 2 (L_commanded): L_cmd = (W/τ) × (1/γ_basket)
             - Labor hours commanded per hour worked
           - Relationship: Φ = L_cmd - 1
           - Labor aristocracy threshold: L_cmd > 1 (equivalently W > τ_effective)
         - Break-even: At W = τ_effective = τ × γ_basket, Φ = 0
         - Key distinction: Imperial rent (extraction) ≠ Class position (wealth percentile)

       C. Basket Visibility (γ_basket)

       Module: src/babylon/economics/melt/basket_visibility.py

       - BasketVisibilityCalculator Protocol
         - Measures imperial subsidy visibility through consumption basket
         - Formula: γ_basket = cost_basket_domestic / cost_basket_international
         - Returns: Dimensionless ratio
         - Data: US Census, BLS expenditure surveys
         - Three-tier validation:
             - Expected range: [0.55, 0.75]
           - Warning range: [0.40, 0.90]
           - Fail range: <0.20 or >1.20

       D. Class Position Classifier
