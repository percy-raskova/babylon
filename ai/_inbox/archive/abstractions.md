Yes — and your instinct about *earned vs. over-engineered* is exactly the right discriminator. The Seam Observatory isn't gold-plating because you can point to a **family** of real incidents it generalizes (silence at the wire). The test for any candidate abstraction is the same: **can you name ≥2 real incidents it would have caught, spanning a recurring failure *class*?** If yes, it's earned. If you're inventing the incident to justify the tool, it's speculative.

The bigger realization hiding in your question: the Seam Observatory is really **instance #1 of a reusable pattern** — *declared-invariant registry + loud sensors + mutation-tested + grows-with-code*. The interesting question isn't "should we build more tooling," it's "**which other engine invariant deserves that same apparatus.**" Here are the ones that have clearly earned it, ranked by how much real pain they generalize:

**1. Determinism Sentinel** — the highest-stakes one, because determinism is *the* constitutional primitive ("every tick produces a deterministic hash; non-determinism is a bug"). Earned incidents: the unseeded `random.random()` in `StruggleSystem` that caused the 3897-vs-3915 divergence; the wall-clock unit test that flaked main; the banned `Date.now()`/`Math.random()` in workflow scripts. `qa:regression` + dense goldens catch divergence *dynamically after the fact*. What's missing is the **static** half: a gate that forbids nondeterminism *sources* in engine code (unseeded RNG, wall-clock, set/dict iteration order, float non-associativity across the libm-transcendental boundary Amendment Q flags) **plus** a double-run-and-compare-hash sensor. This is the most defensible next Sentinel.

**2. State-Conservation / Round-Trip Sentinel** — arguably the *most-recurring* bug family in your history: `from_graph` dropping `institution_relations`, `Territory.county_fips` not round-tripping → the tick-52 crash, the "entities key" mismatch silently discarding data (#46892), and the documented gotcha that `data.get("field", 0.0)` fallbacks *mask* missing-field bugs. This is the Seam Observatory's logic pointed one layer inward — at the `WorldState ↔ graph` boundary instead of the engine↔web wire. A declared registry of round-trip-critical fields + a property test that `from_graph(to_graph(x))` conserves every one, failing loud instead of defaulting to 0.

**3. Economic Conservation / Accounting invariants** — you *already* have a latent branch for this (`053-conservation-invariants`, spared for your ruling). Earned by the `float += Decimal` map-500 and the honest-Φ trap (normalized USE coefficients vs dollars producing garbage `m_j`). Accounting identities that must hold every tick — `K+v+s` balances, value-in = value-out + surplus, the metabolic `ΔB = R − (E·η)` — are exactly where silent numeric drift lives, and they're checkable as invariants.

**4. Wiring / Liveness Sentinel (the general VIII.12 case)** — the Seam's Sensor 2 is a *special case* of this. The broader class: **injected dependencies silently dropped before they reach the computation.** Earned by 7 months of inert parameter-sweep tooling (`run()` ignored the injected overlay), the headless_runner dropping injected `GameDefines`, and CI silently excluding slow tests for want of a `@slow` marker. A gate that proves an injected input is *actually consumed* (generalizing `EconomicsFallbackTally`) would catch this whole family.

**5. Data-Coverage Sentinel** — turn the `NoDataSentinel` fallbacks from silent degradation into a *visible coverage map*. Earned by Φ silently freezing past 2017 (Hickel-ERDI data ends there), LODES rows absent, QCEW suppression. Declare what reference data each computation *requires*; assert availability or degrade *loudly*.

**Already covered (don't rebuild):** layering erosion → `import-linter` (Program 14 is itself this exact species of earned abstraction); repo entropy → `check_repo_hygiene`; divergence-after-the-fact → `qa:regression` + dense goldens.

**One caution consistent with your instinct:** not every invariant earns the *full* apparatus. Round-Trip and Determinism clearly do (recurring, cross-cutting, mutation-worthy). Something like a single formula's `P(S) ∈ [0,1]` bound is better as one property test than a subsystem. The full "registry + sensors + growth" machinery is warranted only when the failure class is **recurring, cross-cutting, and silent** — which is precisely why the seam qualified.

If I had to name the single most-earned next one: **Determinism**, because it's the constitutional bedrock and the goldens only guard it reactively — the static-source half is the gap.
