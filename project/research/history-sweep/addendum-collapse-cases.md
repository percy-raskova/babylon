# Addendum: Four Collapse Cases (Spain/POUM, Paris Commune, Yugoslavia 1948, Greek Civil War)

Follow-up grounding pass for the Epistemic Horizon (fog-of-war) and Doctrine Tree mechanics.
Scope: read-only survey of `/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/` against the
four regions the completeness critic flagged as zero-citation. All quotes below are verbatim
(≤40 words) with exact local file paths. Where a directory was thin, missing, or a linked
"Part" file did not exist on disk, that is stated plainly rather than papered over.

## 1. Evidence per region

### 1.1 Spain / POUM (highest depth)

**Corpus reality check.** `history/spain/poum/` holds only 15 files, not ~102 — a single 1936
bulletin (`1936/no-pasaran.htm`), an index of pre-May-1937 articles running only through
February 1937 (`index.htm`), three PDF issues of *La Révolution Espagnole* (Aug/Oct/Nov 1936,
Apr 1937), and an `images/` folder. `history/etol/document/poum/` holds only `intro.htm`,
`pt1.htm`, and `pt9.htm` of an explicitly multi-part series (Andy Durgan, "The Spanish
Trotskyists and the Foundation of the POUM") — **parts 2–8 are absent**. Neither directory
contains the May 1937 Barcelona events, POUM's internal account of the frame-up, or trial
transcripts. `intro.htm`'s own bibliography names the source that *would* cover the fabrication
mechanics — "*El proceso del POUM: Documentos Judiciales y Policiales* (Barcelona 1989), which
contains the full transcripts and official documents relating to the POUM show trial of 1938" —
but it is not itself mirrored here.

**What is present and strong:** Trotsky's own retrospective analyses, which is where the
Desert-state fabrication and Dissociation-trap material actually lives locally.

- `archive/trotsky/1937/xx/spain01.htm` ("The Lessons of Spain: The Last Warning," Dec 1937) —
  the single richest file for this region. On the fabrication mechanism itself:
  > "the filthy slander; the false documents; the tortures in Stalinist prisons; the murders
  > from ambush – without all this the bourgeois regime under the republican flag could not
  > have lasted even two months."
  Trotsky explicitly frames the GPU's Spain operation as the same apparatus as the Moscow
  Trials, applied to a revolutionary faction to manufacture the political space the Popular
  Front needed. On POUM's own strategic failure (the Dissociation-trap reading the brief asked
  for):
  > "By isolating the revolutionary vanguard from the class, the POUM rendered the vanguard
  > impotent and left the class without leadership."
  and, on why POUM was destroyed despite (not because of) that centrism:
  > "That the POUM nevertheless fell victim to bloody and base repressions was due to the
  > failure of the Popular Front to fulfill its mission... except by cutting off, piece by
  > piece, its own left flank."

- `archive/trotsky/1938/tp/tp-text.htm` (The Transitional Program, 1938) gives the terse
  doctrinal classification:
  > "its own parties (Social Democrats, Stalinists, Anarchists, POUMists) – each in its own way
  > acted as a brake and thus prepared Franco's triumphs."
  Same paragraph names "the judicial frame-ups of Stalin" as the counterpart method to Blum's
  "social" legislation — i.e., Desert-state fabrication is doctrine-canon, not incidental color.

- `history/spain/poum/spanishrevolution/v2n7-apr-21-1937-Spanish Revolution.pdf` (POUM's own
  Barcelona bulletin, April 21, 1937 — **twelve days before the May Days**) is the closest
  local material gets to a contemporaneous POUM voice, and it is valuable precisely because it
  captures the pre-collapse tension without foreknowledge of what was coming:
  > "After twelve days the crisis of the Government of the Generality continues in the same
  > state as on the day it first broke out."
  The same issue reports Public Order Decrees "for disarming the workers" being passed over
  CNT/POUM objection — the disarmament measure that triggered the Telefónica confrontation two
  weeks later is visible in this document as still-contested policy, not yet crisis.

- `archive/trotsky/1939/03/spain02.htm` ("On the Causes for the Defeat of the Spanish
  Revolution," March 1939) was checked and contains **no** POUM/Nin/GPU material — a
  class-character polemic against a Mexican journalist, off-topic despite the promising date.

- The Dewey Commission transcripts (`archive/trotsky/1937/dewey/{intro,session01,session02,
  session12}.htm`) were checked for POUM/Barcelona references and had none — they document
  Trotsky's own Moscow Trials case, a structurally identical fabrication but a different victim,
  so cited only as a doctrinal cousin, not evidence for this region.

- `history/spain/spanishrevolution/v1n18-may-21-1937-span-rev-nyc.pdf` (NYC edition dated exactly
  two weeks after the May Days) exists on disk but **`pdftotext` extracted zero lines** — a
  scanned image with no text layer, unreadable rather than silently skipped.

**Verdict:** strong doctrinal coverage of the fabrication-and-Dissociation-trap themes via
Trotsky; weak-to-absent coverage of event-level mechanics (arrest dates, forged documents,
trial testimony) in this specific mirror.

### 1.2 Paris Commune (medium depth)

`history/france/paris-commune/` has 16 short documents in `documents/`, a single Louise Michel
memoir excerpt (`michel-louise/1886/memories-commune.htm`, 78 lines), and single-file author
folders (Rochefort, Vallès, Trachtenberg-pamphlet PDF).

**Marx's own diagnosis is absent from the mirror.** `archive/marx/works/1871/civil-war-france/`
contains only `news.htm` (a "News Stories" appendix of eyewitness reports on the June 1871
reprisals) and `publication-notes.htm`, which is a live MECW takedown stub:
> "Lawrence & Wishart, who hold the copyright for the Marx Engels Collected Works, have
> directed Marxists Internet Archive to delete all texts originating from MECW... no material
> from MECW is available from marxists.org."
So *The Civil War in France* itself — Marx's actual causal diagnosis, the text the brief asked
for by name — is not retrievable locally. This is the single largest sourcing gap in this
addendum and should be flagged to whoever is grounding the Commune case elsewhere.

**What news.htm does contain** is a real Desert-state fabrication parallel, independent of
POUM: Marx (as the IWA's voice) catches Jules Favre's circular to the European powers hunting
the International red-handed misquoting and misdating IWA documents:
> "he pretended to read extracts from Cabet's pamphlets while reading interpolations of his own
> – a trick exposed while the court was sitting."
This is the *counter-revolutionary* government fabricating evidence against a workers'
organization after a liberated zone's fall — structurally the same move Stalin's GPU makes
against POUM 66 years later, sourced this time from the Versailles side.

**Louise Michel's memoir** (`michel-louise/1886/memories-commune.htm`) is the strongest
"liberated-zone military intelligence" material found here. It captures, in the narrator's own
voice, the exact moment the march-on-Versailles stalls:
> "There is a hill and I shout as I run forward: To Versailles! To Versailles!"
followed immediately by the halt:
> "we're going to spend the night, some of us in the fort, others in the Jesuit convent."
— the offensive converts to static defense at Issy/Clamart/Moulineaux rather than pressing the
March 18 momentum, exactly the "failure to march on Versailles" the brief named. The memoir
also documents federal soldiers finding "regular army soldiers hid in the cellar" of a game
warden's house near Clamart — concrete Versailles infiltration/ambush risk inside the
Commune's own perimeter.

`documents/death-delescluze.htm` (Maxime Du Camp, a hostile witness, flagged as such by the
translator's own note) documents the final 48 hours' internal breakdown:
> "there were no longer either leaders or soldiers; there was nothing but the defeated,
> shipwrecked men who were devouring each other."
Delescluze himself is accused of treason by his own comrades hours before his death at the
barricades — mutual accusation inside the collapsing zone, not just external pressure.

`documents/surrender.htm` is a namesake trap: it is the **Government of National Defense's**
surrender to Prussia (Jan 28, 1871, pre-Commune), not the Commune's own capitulation — it gives
a clean duration for the earlier siege ("The siege of Paris lasted four months and twelve days,
the bombardment a solid month") but must not be conflated with the Commune's own 72-day span.

`documents/circular-international.htm` (1870, pre-Commune) shows the IWA's Paris section
already running its own counter-surveillance apparatus before the Commune existed — "we are
organizing our Vigilance Committees in all neighborhoods" — a Water-state intelligence
precedent, though it predates the Commune proper.

### 1.3 Yugoslavia 1948 (lower depth, corpus genuinely thin)

Confirmed thin exactly as flagged: `subject/yugoslavia/` has only 4 real content files.
`trgo/survey-peoples-liberation-war/index.htm` is a stub whose "Part One/Two/Three" links point
to files **not present on disk** (the WWII Partisan-war survey could not be read, and predates
the split regardless). `ziherl/communism-fatherland/index.htm` links to eight parts of Boris
Ziherl's 1949 polemic *Communism and Fatherland*; only **`ch07.htm` and `ch08.htm` exist
locally** (chapters 1–6 and the conclusion are missing).

`archive/tito/1948/11/26.htm` (Tito's speech to the Slovene Academy, five months after the June
28, 1948 Cominform Resolution) is the strongest primary source found — Tito personally
rebutting the nationalism charge in real time, without yet naming the Cominform outright:
> "I must admit that we find the persistence and unscrupulousness of these attacks on our
> country most extraordinary."
and the doctrinal core of the Yugoslav defense — that "socialism in a single country" is not a
Soviet exclusive:
> "it has seemed quite possible here in Yugoslavia, although it is denied by various
> wiseacres, who thumb tirelessly through the scientific works of Marx, Engels, and Lenin."

`ziherl/communism-fatherland/ch08.htm` (published 1949, referencing events over "the past ten
months," i.e. roughly April 1949) is the cleanest available statement of the trunk-shift
mechanic itself — one Resolution instantly reclassifying an entire faction's legitimacy and
history:
> "the whole of Yugoslav contemporary reality and the whole of the recent past of the Yugoslav
> peoples were turned upside down with one single resolution."
and names the coercive intent explicitly:
> "their national pride must be broken and they must be compelled to surrender pursuant to the
> Cominform resolution, to negate themselves and their deeds."

**No purge/Goli Otok numbers appear anywhere in this local corpus.** I did not find, and am not
asserting, any count of Cominformists arrested, tried, or sent to camps — that data, if needed,
must come from outside this mirror. `ch07.htm` is philosophical (Marxist theory of
cosmopolitanism vs. patriotism) and contains no split-mechanics content beyond footnote dates.

### 1.4 Greek Civil War (lower-medium depth, scattered but usable)

`subject/greek-civil-war/` has the files the brief estimated (~19), spread thin: one 1948 EPON
youth pamphlet, one dated article each for 1949 and 1950, two 1946 *Daily Worker* cartoon
captions, four chapters of Olive Sutton's 1948 pamphlet *Murder Inc. in Greece*
(`murder-inc/{intro,ch07,ch08,ch10}.htm` — chapters 1–6 and 9 are missing), and two **empty**
directories, `fourth-international/1945/` and `/1946/`, despite existing as paths.

The external-intervention thesis is well supported, with real numbers, but with an asymmetry
the brief's framing should register: Sutton's pamphlet (`murder-inc/ch07.htm`) argues the
Athens/US side's own charges of Yugoslav/Albanian/Bulgarian material aid to the Democratic Army
were fabricated —
> "The Yugoslav frontier guards did not open fire on Greek units" — contradicting the Greek
> government's own charge, per the Royalist Third Army Corps' own communique.
Meanwhile aid to the *government* side is concrete and large (`murder-inc/ch10.htm`):
> "the United States government has proclaimed for the world to hear that over $250,000,000
> worth of military equipment has been delivered to the Greek fascists."
And the repression numbers tied directly to the Truman Doctrine's activation
(`murder-inc/ch08.htm`):
> "During June and July, 1947, after the Truman Doctrine went into effect, the arrests and
> deportations were carried out on a grand scale. Over 5,000 were rounded up for exile without
> trial from Athens alone."
followed by "the Athens government arrested 15,000 more men and women" in the following weeks,
and a concentration-camp report that "of 9,000 prisoners arriving from the mainland, 3,000
were trade unionists and labor leaders."

Two later dated articles (both by Pat Sloan in the CPGB's *World News and Views*) bracket the
war's endgame. `1949/03/05.htm` (five months before the actual collapse) states the external
dependency thesis in its cleanest form:
> "The rebels have no chance of total military victory so long as there is a handful of Western
> troops in Salonica and Athens"
and gives a steady-state repression rate:
> "executing its democratic opponents at the rate of two or three a day."
`1950/01/21.htm` (five months after the war's end) gives the postwar prison-camp base rate:
> "Today some 50,000 are in the prisons, in exile or in concentration camps. This would
> correspond, proportionately, to 350,000 people in Britain."

**Notable silence:** neither 1949 article mentions Yugoslavia, Tito, the Cominform, or the 1948
border closure at all, despite writing squarely inside the window when Belgrade's withdrawal of
support was reshaping the KKE's supply situation. This is very likely the CPGB's own
Cominform-loyalist blind spot rather than an oversight — see Tensions, below.

## 2. Coefficient / design implications

Candidate coefficients below are stated as **candidates**, derived only from the quotes above
(1 tick = 1 week per the brief); several are honestly incomplete because the underlying dates
were not present in this mirror.

- **Paris Commune total lifespan:** March 18 – May 28, 1871 = 72 days ≈ **10 ticks**.
- **Paris Commune terminal collapse ("Bloody Week"):** May 21–28 ≈ 8 days ≈ **~1 tick**, first
  Versailles breach of the walls to final surrender/massacre — an extremely fast
  liberated-zone-to-zero transition once the perimeter was actually pierced.
- **Pre-Commune Siege of Paris** (a distinct, earlier event — do not conflate): "four months and
  twelve days" ≈ 132 days ≈ **~19 ticks**, included only as context on how long
  starvation-under-siege was tolerated by the same city before the Commune existed.
- **Yugoslavia trunk-shift:** the Cominform Resolution is a **single-tick, discontinuous** event
  (one document, instant reclassification) — no gradual trust-decay curve is visible; both Tito
  (Nov 1948, +~5 months) and Ziherl (1949, +~10 months) show the rupture still fully unresolved
  at those later checkpoints — a Congress-correction case that **held** rather than collapsed,
  so it likely shouldn't sit in the same collapse-speed table as the other three without caveat.
- **Greek external-intervention repression pulse:** ~20,000 arrested/exiled (5,000 + 15,000) in
  roughly **8 weeks (~8 ticks)** immediately following the Truman Doctrine's activation
  (June–July 1947) — a candidate coefficient for how fast an external patron's material
  commitment converts into a repression spike.
- **Greek postwar repression base rate:** "two or three a day" executions (1949) and a standing
  camp population of 50,000 (Jan 1950, normalized by the source itself to "350,000 people in
  Britain," an explicit ~7x scaling) — usable as a per-tick post-collapse mopping-up coefficient.
- **Spain/POUM:** the only clean local timestamp is "twelve days" of unresolved government
  crisis as of April 21, 1937 — **pre**-collapse tension, not collapse speed. No arrest-to-trial
  timeline for Nin/POUM was found here; a POUM-specific collapse-speed number must come from
  outside this sweep.

## 3. Tensions

1. **Marx's own diagnosis is not retrievable from this mirror.** `civil-war-france/`'s actual
   text was pulled in a 2014 Lawrence & Wishart copyright takedown, leaving only a news-clipping
   appendix and a stub notice. If the engine's design notes anywhere claim to ground the Commune
   case in Marx's own analysis (Versailles infiltration, the failure to seize the Bank of
   France, etc.), that grounding did not come from this local corpus and should be traced to
   wherever it actually originated (possibly the original 45-agent sweep, possibly secondary
   literature) rather than assumed re-derivable from `archive/marx/works/1871/`.

2. **The richest POUM trial evidence is a known gap, not a silent one.** `intro.htm`'s own
   bibliography names the trial-transcript source (`El proceso del POUM: Documentos Judiciales
   y Policiales`) without including it. Local material analyzes the frame-up in general
   theoretical terms (Trotsky) but supplies no specific forged document, arrest date, or trial
   testimony. A Desert-state mechanic wanting a concrete forged-evidence artifact (e.g. the
   actual Orlov/Comorera "invisible-ink map" allegation) needs a source beyond this mirror.

3. **A live cross-region contradiction the sweep should register:** the Greek material here is
   authored almost entirely from Cominform-loyalist positions (CPGB's Pat Sloan, CP-aligned Olive
   Sutton), and **neither 1949 Greek article mentions Yugoslavia, Tito, or the Cominform split**
   — despite writing during the exact window when Belgrade's June 1948 break was cutting off a
   KKE support corridor. This reads as a documented *doctrinal blind spot* rather than a corpus
   gap: partisan reportage on Greece in 1949 is silent on the very external-support collapse the
   Yugoslav case (same sweep) was simultaneously causing. For Epistemic Horizon, this is a clean
   real-world instance of Mass Receptivity/Intel Confidence distortion by doctrinal loyalty — the
   observer class itself has a horizon, not just the player. Part of the answer to "distinguishing
   collapse causes" may be that contemporary reporting itself obscures one of two causes acting on
   Greece at once (external material asymmetry AND external ally attrition via the Tito split).

4. **Nearly all sourced material here is combatant/partisan.** POUM sources are Trotskyist,
   Commune sources are Communard-sympathetic (except the flagged hostile Du Camp excerpt),
   Yugoslav sources are the Titoist state's own organs, Greek sources are pro-KKE. No neutral or
   opposing-side primary source was found for three of the four regions. If Intel Confidence is
   meant to model source reliability, none of today's finds should be treated as ground truth —
   each is a single, committed-observer channel, arguably the right level of unreliability for a
   fog-of-war mechanic, but not to be silently upgraded to "confirmed" downstream.

5. **Greek "external intervention" is asymmetric, not symmetric**, per the local corpus's own
   framing (see §2 table) — the same pamphlet documenting $250M in aid to the government also
   argues the mirror-image charges (foreign aid reaching the DSE) were fabricated. A design that
   assumed a roughly symmetric proxy-war dynamic for Greece should instead model a one-sided
   resource injection, not a two-sided arms race.
