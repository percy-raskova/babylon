## RULING DT-3: Maintenance/Decay (upkeep-decay vs. acquired-forever)

**Question:** does history support per-node TL upkeep with degeneration toward the parent
doctrine absent continued study, or is "acquired forever" (the MVP's current behavior) the
better model?

### (a) CPUSA wartime cadre attrition — what the local corpus actually supports

The local mirror holds only two 1944 CPUSA primary documents
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1944/05/0520-cpusa-convminutes.pdf`
and `.../0522-cpa-constitution.pdf`), both already used in the trunks brief for the top-down
Browder dissolution vote. Neither contains wartime (1941-43) material on cadre being pulled into
war work/military service. **No primary evidence of bottom-up doctrinal drift from cadre
attrition specifically was found in this local mirror** — this sub-claim cannot be verified here
and should not be cited as historically grounded without a different source. The closest
attrition figure is party-history tier (Foster's *History*, via the ProleWiki export): the
mid-1944 Gannett report counted "63,044 members, or 88 percent of those on the rolls of the C.P.
(not counting 15,000 in the armed services)"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_History of the Communist Party of the United States.txt`,
line 2937) — roughly 15,000 cadre physically removed into military service during the exact
period the line drifted. But even this source attributes the drift to Browder's leadership, not
to that attrition, so (a) contributes an attrition *number* without an attrition→drift causal link.

What the corpus *does* support, from a different mechanism, is a **leadership-level curtailment
of internal theoretical/critical practice** as the proximate cause of the Teheran-line drift. The
ProleWiki *History of the Communist Party of the United States* is explicit that the drift was
enabled by a structural failure to keep doing the internal theoretical work, not by rank-and-file
attrition:

> "the violation of democratic centralism went to the opposite, but related extreme, in the
> drastic curtailment of real political discussion, the virtual abolition of self-criticism"
> (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_History of the Communist Party of the United States.txt`, line 2873)

> "The basic reason for this error was the inadequate Marxist-Leninist development of the Party
> and its leaders."
> (same file, line 2867)

This reframes the mechanism from "cadre stopped studying because they were drafted" (unverified
here) to "leadership stopped subjecting the line to discussion/self-criticism, and doctrine
drifted as a direct, stated consequence" — which is functionally the same claim the ruling is
after (coherence requires *ongoing* practice, not a one-time purchase), just with a different
trigger. The drift accumulated over roughly eight years (Browder's "follow Roosevelt" line dates
to 1936-37; the Teheran thesis lands January 1944) before the previously-established 14-month
correction (May 1944 dissolution → July 1945 reconstitution, per the trunks brief).

### (b) erol corpus — study-contingent line-holding

`basoc-20-years.htm` itself does **not** contain the specific "held only as long as X kept
studying/teaching it" claim — its content (already partly used in the trunks brief) is about
"left"/right error diagnosis, not decay mechanics. The stronger find is
`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/erol/ncm-6/lessons.htm`, which states
the maintenance condition directly and in general form:

> "Only a high level of communist consciousness and high degree of theoretical and political
> unity among all cadre can begin to guarantee the correct practice of democratic centralism."
> (`ncm-6/lessons.htm`)

This is conditional language ("can begin to guarantee") — coherent practice is contingent on
*current*, not historical, theoretical unity. The same file's Guardian Clubs case (already cited
in the theoretical-labor brief for a different coefficient) is direct evidence of TL spent with
near-zero effect absent structure: "the clubs lacked a systematic study plan for the entire first
year" (`ncm-6/lessons.htm`) — a full year of nominal TL allocation producing no measurable
coherence gain, which is the mirror image of decay: study without a maintained *plan* doesn't
accrue, so accrued coherence without maintained study shouldn't be expected to persist either.

Three further erol finds state the decay claim directly. The CLP's own 3rd-Congress
self-assessment is a native, in-period theory of upkeep-decay: "the theoretical work in this
regard has just about collapsed and it is only a matter of time before we will begin making
serious mistakes" (`www.marxists.org/history/erol/ncm-2/clp-3rd/section-f.htm`, line 271) —
lapsed theoretical work is expected to *produce future line errors*, not merely a static gap. The
OCIC purge post-mortem names the reversion *target*: cadre with neglected theory "revert to the
guilt ridden liberalism of their class," with "the gross neglect of theoretical development of
the O.C.I.C" judged "to be its undoing" (`ncm-7/mrl-ocic.htm`, line 106) — decayed doctrine slides
toward a prior, ambient class position, not toward zero. And Ignatin's POC memoir supplies the
one concrete duration-to-visible-drift figure in the whole sample: absent from his branch June
1962 to early 1963, on return "Two of the three areas of mass work ... had totally collapsed,"
the drift "directly related to two new theoretical innovations which had been introduced in
those six months" (`1956-1960/ignatin01.htm`, line 96) — **~6 months (≈26 weekly ticks) of
lapsed oversight/study input sufficed for measurable branch-level doctrinal drift.**

One deliberate counter-case bounds the model: the LRS insists its 1980s drift was *not* a
study-hours lapse — "contrary to the perception that the League ceased to study, most new
members have gone through study groups" (`ncm-7/lrs-last-congress-2/appendix.htm`, line 52); the
theory had simply become "not relevant to our politics and practice." Study *quantity* alone does
not guarantee coherence — a caveat any pure upkeep-decay model should carry in its design notes.

### (c) Yan'an Rectification — the cleanest primary evidence in this addendum

Mao's 1945 "On Some Important Problems of the Party's Present Policy" is the single best piece of
primary evidence for upkeep-decay in the entire sweep. It states outright that coherence had been
diluted by growth and *required* an active, multi-year re-education campaign to restore it — it
does not claim doctrine self-repairs:

> "of the members who joined the Party before 1937, only a few tens of thousands are left, and
> most of our present membership of 1,200,000 come from the peasantry"
> (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-3/mswv3_27.htm`)

> "Could we have advanced smoothly if we had not started a widespread movement of Marxist
> education, that is, the rectification movement? Obviously not."
> (same file)

This is structurally identical to the already-known Lenin Levy dilution case (§1.6 of the
theoretical-labor brief) but with the remedy made explicit: dilution is not corrected passively —
it took the 1942-45 Rectification Movement (roughly three years of sustained campaign) to restore
ideological unity. No source in this sweep gives a smooth, per-week numeric decay curve; every
source is qualitative or gives discrete before/after states (diluted → rectified). A Soviet
party-school-specific analogue (line drift when Sverdlov/Comintern-school instructors were purged
or reassigned) was searched for but not located in the local mirror; this sub-claim is unconfirmed.

### Verdict and derivation

**History supports upkeep-decay, not acquired-forever**, but only qualitatively — no source gives
a numeric per-tick decay rate. The two concrete timescales available are: (1) ~8 years (416
ticks) of unaddressed leadership-level drift sufficient to produce a full doctrinal capitulation
(Browder/Teheran), and (2) ~3 years (156 ticks) of sustained active rectification sufficient to
restore coherence after a dilution shock (Yan'an). Treating (1) as the point at which unmaintained
coherence has decayed to a "near-total reversal" state (~90% loss, by analogy to the Lenin-Levy-style
99:1 dilution already used elsewhere in the brief) and solving an exponential decay
`coherence(t) = coherence(0) × exp(−λt)` for `exp(−416λ) = 0.1`:

```
λ = ln(10) / 416 ≈ 2.303 / 416 ≈ 0.00554 per tick   (≈0.55%/week)
```

**RECOMMENDATION:** Adopt upkeep-decay. Give each doctrine node a per-tick coherence decay of
≈0.5-0.6% while `study_allocation` on that node is zero, reverting it toward its parent's
coherence value (not to zero) — consistent with the historical pattern of drift toward a prior,
still-institutionally-present line rather than to nothing. This rate is an **inferred, not
directly historical, number** (derived by anchoring a decay curve to two discrete historical
timescales, not read off a primary source), and should be flagged as playtest-calibrated alongside
the brief's other honestly-uncertain coefficients. The Yan'an case additionally justifies a
*recovery* mechanic: an active `RECTIFICATION` action should be able to restore decayed coherence
over roughly 156 ticks of sustained `study_allocation > 0` on the affected node, rather than
instantly — decay should be reversible but not cheap to reverse, mirroring both the Yan'an
timescale and the brief's existing `doctrine_node_revision_cooldown` "sticky doctrine" pattern.

## RULING DT-4: Study_Allocation Fraction

**Question:** what concrete historical fraction of cadre time went to study vs. other party work?

### What exists locally

`find /media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1935/07/organisers-manual/ -type f`
confirms **only `ch03.htm` exists in this local mirror** — no ch01, ch02, ch04+. "Read all
chapters" is not possible here; ch04 ("Party Membership and Cadres") is linked from ch03's footer
but the file is absent from disk. Ch03 was re-read in full (1,483 lines, not just the excerpt in
the theoretical-labor brief).

### The one real time-budget in the source

Ch03's "HOW SHOULD A UNIT AGENDA (ORDER OF BUSINESS) BE DRAWN UP?" section is the only place in
the chapter with actual minutes/hours attached to an agenda item:

> "A well-organized, well-prepared discussion should not last longer than from one to one and a
> half hours."
> (`.../organisers-manual/ch03.htm`)

> "If the points on the agenda are well prepared, and the proposals are concrete, a Unit meeting
> could easily be finished in no more than two and a half hours."
> (same source — this is the figure already used for `base_unit_weekly_tl_cap` in the
> theoretical-labor brief)

That gives a **1.0-1.5 hr "political discussion" segment out of a ≤2.5 hr meeting — 40-60% of
total meeting time**, the only true agenda-time-fraction in the document. But this segment is
explicitly agitation/current-events analysis, not theoretical study: the worked example is "the
city administration wants to put through a sales tax," and the reporter's job is to explain "how
it will affect the workers... and concrete proposals as to how to mobilize" against it. It is
class-struggle-orientation-in-the-immediate, not classical theoretical education.

Actual theoretical study is explicitly located **outside** this meeting, in a separate
institution the manual names but never time-budgets:

> "To organize open forums, lectures, study circles, workers' schools."
> "To organize training schools for functionaries and study circles for members."
> (both `.../organisers-manual/ch03.htm`, under "WHAT ARE THE TASKS OF THE AGIT-PROP COMMISSION?")

And member time for personal study is explicitly framed as competing with, not scheduled inside,
organizational work — freed up only if organizational burdens are redistributed:

> "leaving more time for study, reading, making friends, and carrying on personal agitation"
> (`.../organisers-manual/ch03.htm`, "WHY ARE MEMBERS OVERBURDENED WITH WORK...")

No duration, cadence, or fraction is ever attached to study circles, workers' schools, or personal
reading time anywhere in ch03.

### Comintern and Lenin School cross-checks

A search of the Comintern's 3rd Congress organizational theses
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/international/comintern/3rd-congress/organisation/note.htm`)
for agenda/hour language returned nothing usable. The Lenin School narrative
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Black Bolshevik.txt`, ch. 7)
confirms the already-known cohort size and course length ("a full three-year course and a short
course of one year," "sixty to seventy qualified students") but gives **no weekly class-hours vs.
other-activity breakdown** for trainees — the only schedule-adjacent detail is that Party Bureau
meetings (organizational, not academic) were held "about once a month," which is not a study-time
figure at all.

### RECOMMENDATION

**The corpus specifies meeting and course *durations* but never a study-time *fraction* — DT-4's
premise is confirmed.** No defensible `Study_Allocation` fraction can be derived from a primary
source in this local mirror; the sweep's own ch03-only reading already exhausted the one
document with real numbers. Do not adopt a number and label it historical.

The closest usable proxy is the 40-60% "political discussion" share of the weekly Unit meeting —
but it must be labeled as **agitation/orientation time, not theoretical study time**, since the
manual keeps those two activities in institutionally separate buckets (Unit meeting vs.
Agit-Prop-run study circles) and never quantifies the latter. Recommend: set `study_allocation`
as a playtest-calibrated default (suggest starting at **0.15-0.25** of total cadre time, on the
reasoning that if political-discussion-plus-organizational-business already consumes 100% of the
capped weekly Unit meeting, dedicated theoretical study circles are a *smaller*, separately-run
add-on activity, not a plurality claim on cadre time) — and flag this starting value explicitly as
an inference from institutional structure, not a historical measurement.

## RULING DT-5: Party Congress Determinism (tag-vector-deterministic vs. seeded RNG)

**Question:** should the Congress "purge of opposed elements" outcome be (a) a deterministic
function of tag-vector deltas, or (b) routed through the seeded tick RNG?

### (1) Peking Review 1959 — the Lushan Plenum was not reported at all

Every locally available 1959 issue around and after the Lushan Plenum (2-16 August 1959) was
checked directly: PR1959-33 (18 Aug), PR1959-34 (25 Aug — cover contents: droughts, industry,
poetry, no China-politics content), PR1959-36a.htm (8 Sept, "A Test of Good Faith" — Soviet nuclear-
test diplomacy only), the PR1959-39 Supplement (Liu Shao-chi's 10th-anniversary speech, 28 Sept —
pure unity/achievement rhetoric, "constantly consolidate and strengthen the great unity of the
Chinese people"), and PR1959-45 (10 Nov, contents list: agriculture, the "Cultural Revolution" in
the arts sense, a railway). **None mention Peng Dehuai, the Plenum's internal struggle, or the
anti-Right-deviation campaign that followed it** — a complete contemporaneous press blackout on
the actual line-struggle resolution. The only public signal was unbroken unanimity messaging,
while the real decision apparatus (Mao's personal reaction to Peng's private letter, closed-door
lobbying) was invisible to any observer, contemporary or otherwise, until the 1981 reversal already
noted in `brief-dt-trunks.md` (a 22-year gap between verdict and correction).

### (2) Peking Review 1976 — the Gang of Four's fall as a fast, contingent event

Mao died 9 September 1976. By PR1976-45 (5 Nov 1976), the resolution is already an accomplished,
unanimous fact: "The Nation Celebrates Great Historic Victory — Mammoth rallies in all provinces...
warmly hail Comrade Hua Kuo-feng as leader... and angrily denounce crimes of the 'gang of four'
anti-Party clique" (table of contents, p.3), with a companion piece describing how Shanghai
"shatter[ed] the scheme of the Wang-Chiang-Yao anti-Party clique to usurp Party and state power"
(p.3). The Gang of Four held major institutional power weeks earlier; the reversal was a sudden
arrest operation (historically dated 6 October 1976, ~4 weeks after Mao's death) — the press record
shows no visible antecedent trend building toward it, only a discontinuous jump from power to
denunciation.

### (3) Yugoslavia 1948 — the one case that looks genuinely deterministic

By contrast, the Tito-Stalin break has a documented, long-building *material* divergence predating
the 1948 Cominform expulsion: Tito "wanted Yugoslavia to accept the Marshall Plan package which
Stalin rejected," and separately wanted "to incorporate Bulgaria and Albania into a Yugoslav
federation" (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/prolewiki/Exports/Main/Josip Broz Tito.txt`, lines
12-14) — two concrete, observable policy-position deltas predating the break by years, not a single
contingent trigger. This is the corpus's best case *for* a deterministic-tag-delta reading.
Corroborating color (not primary, from the same memoir already used for DT-4): the 1922-27
Trotsky-bloc struggle inside the CPSU is described as decided by material/political conditions over
five years of open contestation, not by a single procedural stroke — "he had his day in court and
finally lost because his whole position flew in the face of Soviet and world realities"
(`prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Black Bolshevik.txt`, line 2172) — a second data point, though
retrospective and non-primary, for the deterministic side.

### Synthesis

The trunks brief already established that even RSDLP participants could not foresee the 1903
rupture's axis (Lenin: "the surmise that a rupture may take place... is conjectural in the highest
degree," `archive/lenin/works/1903/2ndcong/9.htm`). This addendum sharpens that into two patterns:
(i) cases where the public/observable record shows **no visible delta at all** before a
discontinuous resolution (Lushan — total blackout; Gang of Four — a four-week coup), and (ii) one
case with a real, measurable material delta predating the break by years (Yugoslavia). "Foreseeable
in hindsight" is true of all three; "determined by measurable prior conditions" is only clearly true
of Yugoslavia — for the two Chinese cases the decisive information was never part of any observable
state to begin with, primary or otherwise.

### RECOMMENDATION (DT-5)

**Pick (b): route the Congress purge outcome through the seeded tick RNG, with tag-vector deltas as
a bias/weight on the roll, not a determining function.** Two of three sampled cases resolve either
invisibly to any observable state or through a fast, discontinuous event with no visible antecedent
trend — neither is expressible as a pure function of tag-vector deltas, since the decisive
information (personal reaction, security-apparatus timing, private correspondence) was never part
of the visible state. Yugoslavia shows real material deltas *can* predict direction, so tag-vectors
should not be discarded: implement `P(purge_succeeds) = f(tag_vector_delta)` feeding a **weighted
seeded-RNG draw**, not a threshold function — large, sustained deltas (à la Yugoslavia) should shift
the odds heavily, but a nonzero contingent term must remain live even at extreme deltas, since even a
dominant faction (Peng Dehuai's pre-Lushan standing, the Gang of Four's pre-October-1976
institutional control) could be, and historically was, reversed by a single contingent event the
visible state did not encode.
