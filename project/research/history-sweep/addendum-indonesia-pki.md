# Addendum: Indonesia/PKI 1965-66 — Epistemic Horizon Calibration Data

Sources are the local read-only mirror at `/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/indonesia/`,
`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/erol/indonesia/`, and a ProleWiki export search. No
git, no pytest — pure historical extraction. All quotes verbatim, ≤40 words, cited to exact local file path.

## 1. Historical evidence

### 1a. Pre-1965 posture: legal mass party, explicit doctrinal silence on armed struggle

The PKI's own 1967 retrospective is the cleanest evidence for the "no Desert-state preparation" hypothesis. It
audits its own 1945, 1959, and 1962 Party programmes and finds that none of them ever authorized armed seizure
of power:

> "did not say a single word on the necessity to seize political power through armed struggle"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/indonesia/1967-PKITheProgramme.htm`)

Worse, the 1959 Sixth Congress programme *explicitly* enshrined the opposite doctrine:

> "the peaceful road or parliamentary road 'is a possibility, a possibility that we must persistently strive to
transform into a reality'"
(same file, quoting the 1959 programme)

The Party calls this out by name as the Khrushchovite "peaceful transition" line and gives an exact duration:

> "which for fourteen years was followed by the former leadership of the Party, is a road leading to catastrophe"
(same file)

Fourteen years spans 1951 (post-Madiun reorganization) to 1965. This is a hard, party-authored confirmation:
the PKI was not merely unlucky — it institutionally declined, for 14 straight years, to build any underground or
armed apparatus, while growing an enormous *legal, aboveground* mass base. From Aidit's 1963 Political Report:

> "there is now a Communist Party of Indonesia which, at the start of this great work had a membership of only
10,000 but which is now more than 2 million strong"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/indonesia/1963-Daring.htm`)

> "more than three-and-a-half million Communists and Communist youths" [Party + Pemuda Rakjat youth wing combined]
(same file)

His January 1959 speech gives an earlier data point on the same trajectory:

> "The membership of the C.P.I, is already about 1,500,000 and almost all of them play an active part"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/erol/indonesia/aidit-basic.pdf`, extracted text)

Mass-front affiliates dwarfed even the Party itself. From Aidit's 1962 Seventh Congress report:

> "the revolutionary peasant organisation BTI, which now has a membership of 4.5 million"

> "Pemuda Rakjat which now has a membership of 1,250,000"
(both `/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/indonesia/1962-AiditForDemocracy.htm`)

So the pre-1965 posture is unambiguous in the local corpus: a legal party growing from 10,000 to 2M+ members
(1951→1963) plus a 4.5M-strong peasant front and 1.25M-strong youth front, publishing membership rolls and
Congress reports openly, with a Party programme that never once names armed insurrection as a task. The
hypothesis is **confirmed**, not refuted, by the PKI's own documents — this was a doctrinal choice sustained for
14 years, not an accident of inexperience (the Party's own history includes the 1926-27 anti-colonial revolt and
the 1948 Madiun affair, so underground/armed action was not outside institutional memory — it was deliberately
set aside).

### 1b. Rollup speed and scale

The local corpus does not contain a single clean "before/after" casualty table, but it brackets the collapse
with two dated data points. An eyewitness account from a foreign (pro-PKI) visitor who was in Jakarta 13-23
October 1965, published two months later:

> "there have been perhaps as many as 30,000 progressives arrested by the army generals of the coup"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/erol/indonesia/alley-coup.htm`, *The People's Voice*
[NZ], 15 Dec 1965)

The same witness dates the pre-coup mass mobilization for scale comparison:

> "as far back as May of this year, there were already as many as 5,000 peasant actions a day in Java"
(same file)

Two years later, the Party's own November 1967 programme gives the only order-of-magnitude tally found locally
for the full toll:

> "The bloody experience in which hundreds of thousands of Communists and other patriotic fighters have been
killed"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/indonesia/1967-PKITheProgramme.htm`)

Taken together: a party of 2,000,000+ aboveground members (last locally-sourced snapshot: 1963) suffers "as many
as 30,000" arrests within roughly the first ten days of the crackdown, and by the Party's own 1967 accounting
the toll has reached "hundreds of thousands" killed. What replaces the destroyed legal party, per the same
document, is not a rebuilt mass party but a from-scratch rural guerrilla project:

> "through the winning of victories locality by locality, through the liberation of the countryside from feudal
oppression"
(same file, describing the *new* 1967 strategy — i.e., starting over)

No document in the local mirror gives a rump/surviving-membership figure. The organizational collapse is
total enough that the 1967 programme has to re-derive strategy from zero rather than describe reconstituting the
old apparatus.

### 1c. The PKI's own doctrinal post-mortem

The single most important primary document — the September 1966 "Self-Criticism of the Political Bureau of the
CC PKI" (titled *Build the PKI Along the Marxist-Leninist Line to Lead the People's Democratic Revolution in
Indonesia*) — is **not present in the local mirror**. It is linked by filename
(`1966-SudismanOtoKritik.htm`) from the index page
`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/indonesia/1971-PKIFiveDocuments.htm`, along with three
sibling documents (`1966-PKIHoldAloft.htm`, `1966-PKITakeTheRoad.htm`, `1967-PKIHoldAloft.htm`) — all four are
dead links in this mirror; confirmed absent via directory search. This is a real gap, flagged rather than papered
over.

The one verbatim fragment of the Self-Criticism recoverable locally comes second-hand, quoted inside a November
1968 Peking Review article reprinting an Indonesian Delegation statement:

> "the military dictatorship of the Right-wing army generals which is now in power is also a paper tiger"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/erol/indonesia/pr-44.pdf`, extracted text, quoting
"Self-Criticism by the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Indonesian Communist Party")

The doctrinal content of the post-mortem is otherwise reconstructable from the 1967 Programme's introduction and
body (Section 1a above) and the 1971 collection's own framing, which names the Self-Criticism's prescriptive fix
as the "New Three Banners": (1) a Marxist-Leninist party purged of "subjectivism, opportunism and modern
revisionism," (2) "armed People's struggle of the peasants... under the leadership of the working class," (3) a
"revolutionary united front based on the worker-peasant alliance." In the PKI's own retrospective vocabulary, the
trap was named explicitly as Soviet-derived "modern revisionism" expressed as the "peaceful road" / parliamentary
road, sustained 1951-1965.

### 1d. Falsification / deception dynamics

The local corpus does not contain a document specifically analyzing the "September 30th Movement" (G30S)
evidentiary controversy, but two things are recoverable. First, the PKI's own retrospective framing treats G30S
as an exploited pretext rather than owning it as party action:

> "taking the 'September 30th Affair' as a pretext, completely took over the state power"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/indonesia/1967-PKITheProgramme.htm`)

Second, the eyewitness account describes a concrete manufactured-evidence technique — fabricated "spontaneous"
popular anger, produced under military curfew and attributed to the army/police:

> "It was on those nights that the walls of compounds and buildings facing the streets were painted with all
manner of insulting slogans"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/erol/indonesia/alley-coup.htm`)

> "Certainly the generals' coup in Indonesia showed all the sign of adequate financing and careful preparation"
(same file)

These are partisan sources (a PRC-aligned New Zealand paper, and the PKI's own exile leadership) — useful for
capturing the self-perceived deception dynamic (state fabricates evidence of mass rage to legitimize a
pre-planned purge), not neutral historiography.

## 2. Coefficient/design implications (1 tick = 1 week)

**Doctrine debt as a hidden multiplier, not a visible stat.** The 14-year (1951-1965 → ~728 ticks) sustained
"peaceful road" doctrine is the mechanism, not the repression event itself. Candidate: an Organization holding a
`peaceful_transition` / legal-mass-party doctrine flag accrues a *Desert-unpreparedness debt* that scales with
ticks-held-under-doctrine and is invisible to the player/AI narrator until a Rupture-adjacent event tests it. This
matches Amendment Q's "loud failure, not gradual leak" framing: the debt is silent until catastrophic, exactly
like the PKI case.

**Two-speed collapse, not one exponential.** The local evidence brackets two distinct timescales that argue
against modeling annihilation-scale repression as a single smooth decay curve:
- *Shock phase*: ~30,000 arrests inside roughly the first 1-2 ticks (10 days) of the triggering event — a
  near-instantaneous leadership/cadre decapitation spike.
- *Grinding phase*: the Party's own tally of "hundreds of thousands killed" is reported from a ~2-year (~104-tick)
  vantage (Nov 1967 looking back to Oct 1965); the local corpus does not give a finer-grained weekly or monthly
  breakdown within that window, so the exact width of the "grinding phase" itself is not directly sourced here —
  only the two endpoints (10-day shock count; 2-year retrospective total) are.
Candidate mechanic: a Desert-transition triggered by annihilation-scale repression should apply a **cliff-function**
to Organization membership/Intel Confidence in the first 1-2 ticks (order-of-magnitude drop), followed by a
long-tail attrition floor, rather than one continuous curve.

**Mass Receptivity (M_r) must be decoupled from survival capacity.** This is the single clearest design lesson.
PKI's M_r-analog was at or near ceiling — 2M+ Party members, 4.5M BTI peasants, 1.25M Pemuda Rakjat youth, all
organized *legally and openly* — yet this produced **zero** defensive value against annihilation-scale
repression, because none of it had been converted into underground/armed capacity. Candidate coefficient:
**Desert Conversion Ratio** = fraction of aboveground mass base convertible to hidden/armed resistance capacity
when Intel Confidence collapses. The PKI case anchors the *worst-case floor* of this ratio at effectively 0 — the
1967 New Programme has to rebuild armed capacity "locality by locality" from scratch rather than convert an
existing 2M+ base, which is the strongest available evidence that M_r and survival-under-annihilation are
orthogonal, not correlated, variables. A design that lets high M_r substitute for missing Hardness/Desert-prep
would misrepresent this case.

**Intel Confidence (I_c) should crash asymmetrically to the collapse.** Pre-1965, the party's own programme
language ("we must persistently strive to transform [the peaceful road] into a reality") reflects an I_c reading
of state intent that, in hindsight, was catastrophically miscalibrated — the org believed the parliamentary path
was viable up to the moment its cadre were being rounded up. Candidate: under a `peaceful_transition` doctrine
flag, I_c should be modeled as *doctrine-locked* (insensitive to rising real repression signal) rather than
Bayesian-updating, and should only unlock/crash once the annihilation-scale event has already begun — i.e., the
mechanic should allow an organization to walk into the Desert with a *falsely high* I_c reading right up to the
trigger, which is exactly what the sourced programme language shows.

**Doctrine Tree "trap" naming.** The PKI's own vocabulary gives a ready-made label: the trap is not generic
"reformism" but specifically the **Khrushchovite peaceful-transition / parliamentary-road line**, and the
prescribed antidote in the Party's own words is the "New Three Banners" — ideological vanguard purity, armed
peasant war under working-class leadership, and a worker-peasant united front. This is a clean, source-grounded
name and fix-vector for whatever node the Doctrine Tree currently uses for a legalist/parliamentary trap.

**Falsification/false-flag as an I_c-corruption input, not just a repression multiplier.** The manufactured
night-time slogans (described as attributable to army/police under curfew) and the "pretext" framing of G30S
suggest the mechanic should let a hostile state actor *inject* fabricated mass-sentiment signal that corrupts an
org's or an AI narrator's read of M_r during the shock window — not just apply raw repression force.

## 3. Tensions

**Source partisanship.** Every local document is either PKI-authored (party programmes, Aidit's own speeches,
the exile Delegation's 1971 framing) or fraternal-party press (Peking Review, a PRC-aligned NZ paper). Membership
figures (10,000→2M+, BTI 4.5M, Pemuda Rakjat 1.25M) are self-reported Congress-report numbers, not independently
audited; the "hundreds of thousands killed" figure is the Party's own 1967 accounting, not a neutral tally. None
of this makes the numbers false, but the corpus cannot itself corroborate them — treat these as the PKI's
*self-model*, which is exactly what the Epistemic Horizon mechanic needs (an org's own miscalibrated I_c/M_r
reading), but do not treat it as a validated casualty count for any other purpose.

**The single most important document is missing.** The actual September 1966 Self-Criticism text — the
document every other file in this collection treats as the turning point — is a dead link in this mirror. What
we have is a two-sentence secondhand quotation embedded in someone else's polemic (`pr-44.pdf`), plus extensive
paraphrase/summary from the *later* 1971 collection's introduction and the 1967 Programme's own recap of it. If
tighter doctrinal calibration is wanted later, sourcing the actual Self-Criticism text (it is a well-known,
independently digitized document outside this specific local mirror) would upgrade this from secondhand
paraphrase to primary quotation — that upgrade was not done here since it falls outside the read-only local
corpus this pass was scoped to.

**Four PDFs are unreadable stubs, not absence of evidence.** `pr-22.pdf`, `pr-67-35.pdf`, and both 1958 Plenum
PDFs (66 pp. + 20 pp.) are image scans with no OCR text layer; `pdftotext` returned nothing from any of them.
These likely contain exactly the kind of pre-1965 Plenum-level organizational detail (branch counts, cadre
structure, internal debate over the parliamentary line) that would sharpen the "no Desert-state prep" hypothesis
beyond the Congress-report level evidence used above — they were not silently skipped, but they are unread, and
OCR would be needed to close this gap.

**No single before/after membership pair.** The last locally-sourced membership snapshot is 1963 (2M+ Party,
3.5M+ combined with youth), not a 1965-immediately-pre-coup figure, and no document gives a surviving/rump count
after the destruction — only qualitative "hundreds of thousands killed" and the 1967 Programme's implicit
admission of starting over. Any "before/after" game coefficient built from these numbers is therefore
extrapolating across a ~2-year gap on the "before" side and inferring a near-zero floor (rather than reading a
sourced number) on the "after" side — this should be stated as an inferred floor, not a directly cited figure, if
it ends up in a coefficients table.

**Doctrinal choice vs. institutional innocence.** The 1967 Programme frames the absence of armed-struggle
doctrine as 14 years of *chosen* revisionist line, not organizational inexperience — the Party's own history
includes the 1926-27 revolt and the 1948 Madiun affair, both non-legal/armed episodes within institutional living
memory (Aidit's 1960 speech, `1960-AiditLeninandIndonesia.htm`, discusses the post-Madiun 1951 rebuild directly).
This matters for calibration: the "no Desert-state preparation" hypothesis should be modeled as a *doctrine
decision an org can make and unmake*, not a fixed trait of orgs that grow large legally — the PKI had the
organizational memory to do otherwise and, per its own self-criticism, chose the parliamentary road anyway for
14 years running.
