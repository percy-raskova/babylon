# Design Brief: Theoretical Labor Economics (Doctrine Tree mechanic)

Theme: study circles, party schools, party press, cadre training — costs in
cadre-time, returns, decay of theoretical coherence.

## 1. Historical evidence

### 1.1 Cadre-time is a real, measured opportunity-cost draw, not a flavor stat

The clearest primary-source statement of Theoretical Labor as an *opportunity
cost* rather than a free stat comes from Lenin himself: socialist consciousness
"is something introduced into the proletarian class struggle from without and
not something that arose within it spontaneously" (quoting Kautsky approvingly)
— `archive/lenin/works/1901/witbd/ch02.htm`. This is the founding axiom of a
Scientific/vanguard trunk: doctrine must be *purchased* with dedicated labor,
it does not accrue automatically from struggle. Mao's formulation is blunter
still: "Cadres are a decisive factor, once the political line is determined"
(`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-2/mswv2_10.htm`) — doctrine is
inert without a cadre stock able to execute it, directly supporting a
multiplicative `TL = cadre_count × study_allocation × coherence` formula
rather than an additive one.

Lenin's own retrospective periodization of the RSDLP gives a hard number for
how long pure theory-building can run before mass payoff: "The first period
embraces about ten years, approximately from 1884 to 1894 ... the rise and
consolidation of the theory and programme of Social-Democracy," followed by a
~4-year mass-upsurge period, 1894-98
(`archive/lenin/works/1901/witbd/concl.htm`). That is a **2.5:1 ratio of
theory-investment years to mass-payoff years** — direct primary-source
evidence for a long horizon on TL return.

Theoretical Labor also gets *zeroed out* under acute crisis. In the Postscript
to *State and Revolution* (Nov 1917), Lenin abandons a planned final chapter
outright: "It is more pleasant and useful to go through the 'experience of
revolution' than to write about it"
(`archive/lenin/works/1917/staterev/postscpt.htm`) — TL allocation dropped to
0% the moment October hit, and stayed there permanently for that text. The
book itself took roughly two months to write (Aug-Sept 1917) across six
chapters before the crisis interrupted it
(`archive/lenin/works/1917/staterev/preface.htm`), and the finished doctrine
proved *sticky*: the second edition, ~16 months later, added exactly one
section (`archive/lenin/works/1917/staterev/preface.htm`) — real doctrine,
once purchased, resists cheap revision.

A single emergency theoretical mobilization at the very top of a movement
cost roughly nine cadre-months: Lenin's *Materialism and
Empirio-Criticism* against Bogdanov/Machism "was the outcome of a prodigious
amount of creative scientific research carried out by Lenin during nine
months," citing 200+ source titles
(`archive/lenin/works/1908/mec/index.htm`) — and even that output was taxed:
Lenin "had to agree to tone down some passages ... so as not to give the
tsarist censorship an excuse for prohibiting its publication," a real
repression-friction term on TL fidelity (same source).

### 1.2 Cadre headcounts and cadre:mass ratios are tabulated in the primary record

The CPUSA's own 1930 Language Bureau fraction-coverage report gives concrete,
tiered cadre-to-mass leverage: "The factions ... numbering almost 5,000 Party
members, control organizations having about 50,000 members. About 800 Party
members work among 140,000 workers in organizations in which we have
influence"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1930/0600-cpusa-languagework.pdf`).
That is **~1:10 cadre:mass in fully-controlled orgs vs. ~1:175 in merely
influenced ones** — a real, tabulated ratio for how cadre presence should
scale a mass-relationship stat at different control levels.

The United Communist Party of America's November 1920 financial statement
lets us derive a concrete cadre count from a wage bill: summed national,
local, and editorial payroll of $4,528 implies "a payroll of 25.2 full-time
individuals" at $45/week average salary, against a nationwide dues-paying
membership of 5,801
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1921/0102-ucp-conventionminutes.pdf`)
— **roughly 1 paid FTE cadre per 230 members**.

Mao's 1938 Sixth Plenum sets an absolute floor for coherent-cadre capacity: "our
Party's fighting capacity will be much greater ... if there are one or two
hundred comrades with a grasp of Marxism-Leninism which is systematic and not
fragmentary" (`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-2/mswv2_10.htm`) —
**100-200 deeply-trained cadre** judged sufficient to lead a
several-hundred-million-person movement. A smaller, earlier version of the
same threshold: the pre-1903 St. Petersburg League of Struggle formed by
merging "about 20 Marxist workers circles"
(`history/erol/ncm-1a/premises.htm`), and Mao's own 1955-56 "Ten Great
Relationships" doctrine was drafted after consulting "over thirty cadres"
(`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-8/mswv8_64.htm`) — three
concrete "minimum viable doctrine-drafting cohort" sizes: 20 (founding), 30
(major-line consultation), 100-200 (movement-leading coherent core).

### 1.3 Party press: circulation, cost-splits, and cadence are directly quantified

The CPUSA's 1935 *Organizer's Manual* states the movement's own ROI math for
agitation: "If each neighborhood paper would be read by only 200 or 300
people we would have close to a million workers closely connected with the
Party"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1935/07/organisers-manual/ch03.htm`)
— a **200-300 reader-per-unit multiplier scaling to ~1,000,000 aggregate
mass contact**. The same manual fixes base-unit cadence: "Only in exceptional
cases ... should we make exceptions from the rule of one meeting per week for
each Unit," capped near 2.5 hours (same source) — the smallest, hardest
historical unit of TL/organizational overhead.

The UCP's November 1920 books split mass-facing press spend from
internal-doctrine spend: "Official Organ 976.17 ... E.L.O. [*The Toiler*]
2691.23"
(`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1921/0102-ucp-conventionminutes.pdf`)
— **a ≈2.76:1 ratio favoring external agitation press over internal cadre
formation**, against total monthly party expenses of $15,735.98. Lenin's 1902
"Reply to a Reader" sets the historical floor for calling something a real
periodical rather than occasional leaflets: "appearing, not once, but at
least two or four times, a month"
(`archive/lenin/works/1902/feb/01b.htm`) — **2-4 issues/month minimum
cadence**.

Growth-curve data across the 20th century gives a real TL-to-circulation
growth-rate baseline: *The Call* went from ~5,000/month to ~15,000/week over
five years (`history/erol/ncm-3/call-5th.htm`); a 1976 push converted it from
monthly to weekly inside about six months, generating 120 submitted
articles/month and committees in 30 cities
(`history/erol/ncm-3/call-weekly-2.htm`); *The Guardian*'s circulation
baseline sat near 25,000 with a doubling target requiring sustained organized
effort, not passive growth (`history/erol/ncm-4/silber-clubs.htm`); and *The
Call*'s actual audience composition was measured directly — "75% of the
newspapers are sold to working-class and minority people," with ~50%
distributed at factory gates (`history/erol/ncm-3/ol-call-conf.htm`). At the
high end, the Soviet 1954 *Political Economy* textbook's first edition ran
**6,000,000+ copies**, explicitly for "Party schools and study-groups"
(`prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Political Economy.txt`) — the study-circle
material scale ceiling for a mature, mass state party. By contrast, an
under-resourced sect (LPR) produced only 53 articles/year with <2% touching
the national question, averaging roughly one propaganda article/week — a
directly cited case of "propaganda to the advanced" substituting for
mass-facing agitation, correlated with **seven years of sectarian plateau**
(`history/erol/ncm-1a/atm-denver-speech.htm`).

### 1.4 Cadre training duration and tiering

Comintern-run elite formation had real multi-year duration: the Lenin School
(Moscow, 1926-29) trained "sixty to seventy qualified students" per cohort on
a 1-year short course or full 3-year course
(`prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Black Bolshevik.txt`). Stalin's 1937
plenum mandated a tiered course system scaled to cadre seniority — "four-month
Party courses ... in each regional center," with 6- and 8-month tiers for
higher strata, against leadership-population tiers of 3,000-4,000 (top),
30,000-40,000 (middle), and 100,000-150,000 (lower rank)
(`prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_On the shortcomings of party work and measures to eliminate Trotskyist and other double-dealers_Our Tasks.txt`).
The whole Bolshevik cadre-formation project outside Russia took roughly five
years (1918-1923) to produce its first mature leadership fruits
(`subject/stalinism/origins-future/ch1-2a.htm`).

### 1.5 Congresses, control commissions, and rectification are themselves costed institutions

The 10th RSDLP(b) Congress (16 March 1921) fixed the Control Commission's
size precisely: "the commission is elected with a membership of seven full
members and three candidate members" at the center, 3-5/2-3 at oblast level,
3/2 at guberniia level (`history/ussr/government/party-congress/10th/16c.htm`)
— with an explicit opportunity-cost rule that "a certain portion of the
members ... are to be freed from all other work" (same source), plus
seniority gates: 10 years' membership for the central tier, 5 years for
oblast, membership "since Feb 1917" for guberniia (same source). Party Rules
adopted in 1903 set the baseline Congress cadence: "The congress is convened
(if possible not less than once in two years)"
(`archive/lenin/works/1904/council/6viii.htm`) — but the actual gap between
the December 1908 Conference and the January 1912 Prague Conference ran
roughly 37 months, "the necessity for intensive work to re-establish the
illegal organisation of the R.S.D.L.P." cited as the cause
(`archive/lenin/works/1912/6thconf/2.htm`) — **~50% cadence slippage under
sustained state repression**. The modern CPC Constitution (2002) fixes
National Congress cadence at 5 years and Central Committee turnover at "not
exceed one-fifth" per cycle
(`prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Constitution of the Communist Party of China (2002).txt`);
the Workers' Party of Korea's Sixth Congress (1980) was preceded by roughly a
year of explicit preparation, with six congresses across 35 years implying a
~5.8-year average cadence (`prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Kim Jong Il_ Brief History.txt`).

Self-criticism carried a real, numeric pass/fail bar in practice: 1949 land-reform
convention practice asked "do the achievements amount to 30 per cent or to 70
per cent of the whole?"
(`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-4/mswv4_59.htm`) — work was
"in the main approved" only above a ~70% achievement threshold. The
Revolutionary Workers League (1976) modeled a distinct partial-credit rule
for absorbing even hostile criticism: "We will instead seek to find the 5%
which is correct" (`history/erol/ncm-1a/rwl-history.htm`).

### 1.6 Coherence dilution and TL waste states

Mass recruitment can be weaponized to dilute coherence rather than build it.
The 1924 "Lenin Levy" admitted 250,000 new members in three months
specifically "to be used as a battering ram against the Left Opposition"
(`subject/stalinism/origins-future/ch1-1a.htm`); as a result "only 1 per cent
of the Bolshevik Party of 1924 had been members before March 1917"
(`subject/stalinism/origins-future/ch1-1a.htm`) — a **99:1 dilution of
pre-revolutionary cadre by post-revolutionary recruits**, directly modeling a
coherence-divisor collapse triggered from above. Theoretical Labor can also be
nominally allocated yet functionally near-zero: the 1977 Guardian Clubs "lacked
a systematic study plan for the entire first year"
(`history/erol/ncm-6/lessons.htm`) — a full year of TL spend at a near-zero
coherence multiplier. And TL divorced from investigation produces "stereotyped"
output: Mao's 1942 "Oppose Stereotyped Party Writing" ties dogmatism directly to
theory purchased without paired mass investigation
(`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-3/mswv3_07.htm`), with Stalin's
terse, short 1941 war speech offered as the positive counter-example in the same
text — verbose doctrinal output is a *tell* of low coherence, not a sign of
investment.

## 2. Design recommendations

All coefficients below assume a **weekly game tick** (matching the historical
base-unit cadence of one meeting/week, §1.1/1.3) unless noted.

**TL formula core.** Retain `TL = cadre_count × study_allocation × coherence`
as specified; the historical record supports this as multiplicative, not
additive (Mao: doctrine inert without cadre stock, §1.1; Lenin: consciousness
must be actively imported, not spontaneous, §1.1).

**`cadre_fte_cost_per_member`** — derivation: UCP Nov 1920, 25.2 FTE / 5,801
members ≈ **0.00434 FTE/member** (≈1 FTE per 230 members)
(`.../1921/0102-ucp-conventionminutes.pdf`). Recommend as the *baseline*
cadre-maintenance cost define for an underground/pre-legalization party
stage; historically-grounded, no playtest scaling needed at this stage since
it's a direct ratio, not a rate.

**`mass_reach_multiplier` (controlled vs. influenced orgs)** — derivation:
CPUSA 1930, controlled ≈1:10, influenced ≈1:175
(`.../1930/0600-cpusa-languagework.pdf`). Recommend two discrete
`org_control_tier` multipliers — `CONTROLLED = 10.0`, `INFLUENCED = 175.0`
mass-per-cadre — feeding directly into Intel Confidence's cadre-presence term
in the Epistemic Horizon mechanic (cross-mechanic coupling; both mechanics
draw on the same cadre stock).

**`agitation_reader_multiplier`** — derivation: CPUSA 1935 Organizer's
Manual, 200-300 readers/paper unit → ~1,000,000 aggregate contact
(`.../1935/07/organisers-manual/ch03.htm`). Recommend
`AGITATE_LOCALLY` yield = `readers_per_unit (200-300) × active_press_units`,
starting value 250 (midpoint); this is a real historical conversion, not a
guess, but the *aggregate* 1,000,000 figure was the Party's own optimistic
projection, not a measured outcome — flag the aggregate multiplier itself as
needing playtest damping (the source is aspirational arithmetic, not an
audited result).

**`base_unit_weekly_tl_cap`** — derivation: CPUSA 1935 Manual, 1
meeting/week, ≤2.5 hours (`.../1935/07/organisers-manual/ch03.htm`).
Recommend: one `STUDY_CIRCLE` action per cadre-unit per weekly tick, hard cap;
this is the smallest, best-attested historical unit and should be a hard
per-tick ceiling on TL draw per unit, not merely a soft default.

**`press_split_ratio` (mass-agitation : internal-doctrine spend)** —
derivation: UCP Nov 1920 books, Toiler $2,691.23 : Official Organ $976.17 ≈
**2.76:1** (`.../1921/0102-ucp-conventionminutes.pdf`). Recommend TL's "study
allocation" sub-budget split roughly 73%/27% between a mass-press node and an
internal-doctrine-organ node by default, adjustable by player but anchored to
this historical baseline rather than an even 50/50 split.

**`min_periodical_cadence`** — derivation: Lenin 1902, 2-4 issues/month
(`archive/lenin/works/1902/feb/01b.htm`). Recommend: a Party Press doctrine
node only counts as "active" (eligible for circulation growth) if it fires
≥2 times per game-month (≈ every 2 weekly ticks); below that it should be
modeled as inert leafleting, consistent with the LPR sectarian-plateau
counter-case (§1.3).

**`tl_theory_to_payoff_ratio`** — derivation: Lenin's own periodization,
~10 years theory-building : ~4 years mass payoff ≈ **2.5:1**
(`archive/lenin/works/1901/witbd/concl.htm`). Recommend: TL invested in a
foundational/trunk-level doctrine node should not begin yielding Mass
Receptivity gains for roughly 2.5x as many ticks as were spent accumulating
it — a genuine multi-tick lag, not an instant unlock. This is a ratio derived
from one case (Russian Social-Democracy 1884-98); flag for playtest
calibration on absolute tick-length, since the *ratio* is well-evidenced but
the absolute duration is era/context-specific.

**`major_doctrine_node_cost`** — derivation: Lenin's *Materialism and
Empirio-Criticism*, ~9 cadre-months for one major counter-doctrine text
(`archive/lenin/works/1908/mec/index.htm`). Recommend: a Scientific-trunk
"major theoretical work" node costs ≈9 cadre-weeks of a single high-coherence
cadre-unit (converting cadre-months 1:1 to game-weeks under the standard
weekly tick), pulling that cadre-unit out of all other action slots for the
duration — a genuine, blocking opportunity-cost draw, not a background tax.

**`doctrine_node_revision_cooldown`** — derivation: *State and Revolution*,
1 substantive addition across ~16 months between editions
(`archive/lenin/works/1917/staterev/preface.htm`). Recommend: once purchased,
a doctrine node should be effectively locked (no cheap re-edit) for ≈16
weeks (mapping months≈weeks at 1:1 under the weekly tick, since no finer
historical grain exists) — this makes doctrine "sticky" against opportunistic
low-cost edits, directly opposing a hypothetical cheap re-roll exploit.

**`crisis_tl_override`** — derivation: Lenin abandoning *State and
Revolution*'s Chapter 7 entirely at the October crisis
(`archive/lenin/works/1917/staterev/postscpt.htm`). Recommend: a discrete
`ACUTE_CRISIS` world-state flag that sets `study_allocation = 0` for the
duration of the crisis tick(s) — TL should not be a smoothly-taxed resource
under crisis, it should hit a hard floor, matching the historical
instant-to-zero behavior rather than a gradual decay curve.

**`control_commission_size`** — derivation: 10th RSDLP Congress, 7 full + 3
candidate at center tier, seniority gates 10y/5y/since-Feb-1917
(`history/ussr/government/party-congress/10th/16c.htm`). Recommend a
Party-Congress "rectification tribunal" sub-body sized 7 (voting) + 3
(non-voting) cadre, gated by an in-game `cadre_seniority_ticks` stat at three
tiers proportional to those historical ratios (top-tier gate ≈2x the
mid-tier gate ≈2x the base gate — 10:5:~4 in the historical case, scaled to
whatever the game's typical cadre lifespan turns out to be in playtest).

**`congress_base_cadence` / `congress_repression_slippage`** — derivation:
RSDLP Rules mandate 2 years; observed actual gap (Dec 1908→Jan 1912) ≈37
months under sustained repression, a ~50% slippage
(`archive/lenin/works/1912/6thconf/2.htm`,
`archive/lenin/works/1904/council/6viii.htm`). Recommend: default Party
Congress (trunk_shift-capable) cooldown = 2 years (104 weekly ticks); under a
`high_repression` world-state flag, multiply the cooldown by 1.5x (i.e.
allow it to slip toward the historically-observed ~3-year real gap) rather
than blocking Congresses outright. The CPC's later, more stable constitutional
cadence of 5 years (`.../Library_Constitution of the Communist Party of China (2002).txt`)
is offered as an alternative "mature, legal-party" default if the design
wants a slower baseline once a faction reaches legal/majority status —
flagged as a design choice, not resolved by history alone (see §3).

**`self_criticism_pass_threshold`** — derivation: 1949 land-reform convention
convention, 70% achievement bar for "in the main approved"
(`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-4/mswv4_59.htm`). Recommend:
a `self_criticism` Congress action succeeds (restores coherence, avoids
escalation to trap) if the target doctrine node's retained-correct fraction
is ≥0.7; below that, model a partial/censured outcome rather than binary
pass/fail, consistent with the RWL 5%-partial-credit precedent
(`history/erol/ncm-1a/rwl-history.htm`) for even largely-wrong criticism.

**`coherence_dilution_from_recruitment`** — derivation: Lenin Levy, 250,000
admitted in ~3 months, pre-1917 cadre share collapsing to 1%
(`subject/stalinism/origins-future/ch1-1a.htm`). Recommend: any single-tick
cadre-count increase exceeding roughly 10x the prior cadre stock (the Lenin
Levy was on that order relative to the pre-levy party) should apply an
immediate, steep coherence-divisor penalty rather than a smooth per-capita
average — mass admission is a discrete shock to coherence, not a gentle
dilution.

**`minimum_viable_doctrine_cohort`** — derivation: three concrete historical
cohort sizes — 20 circles merged (pre-1903 St. Petersburg League,
`history/erol/ncm-1a/premises.htm`), ~30 cadre consulted (Ten Great
Relationships, 1955-56,
`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-8/mswv8_64.htm`), and 100-200
deeply-trained cadre to lead a nationwide movement (1938 Sixth Plenum,
`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-2/mswv2_10.htm`). Recommend
tiering doctrine-node purchase eligibility: a *founding* doctrine node needs
≥20 cadre-units contributing TL that tick; a *major line* node needs ≥30; a
*trunk-defining* node needs 100-200 — three real, sourced floor values rather
than one arbitrary minimum.

## 3. Tensions and open questions

- **Congress cadence has no single historical answer.** The RSDLP's own rule
  (2 years) slipped 50% under repression to ~3 years
  (`archive/lenin/works/1912/6thconf/2.htm`); the CPC's later constitutional
  cadence is 5 years; the WPK averaged 5.8 years; CPI (Maoist) runs a
  three-tier 5/3/2-year system. History gives a *range* (2-6 years) rather
  than one number, and the range correlates with regime maturity (underground
  vs. legal vs. state party) more than with any universal constant. The
  brief's recommendation (2-year base + repression multiplier) picks the
  earliest, most volatile end of that range because Babylon's premise is a
  contesting, not-yet-victorious movement — this is a design choice, flag for
  owner confirmation.
- **The Organizer's Manual's "1,000,000 reach" figure is the Party's own
  optimistic projection, not an audited outcome** (`.../organisers-manual/ch03.htm`).
  It is legitimate evidence of *how the movement itself modeled ROI*, which is
  useful for flavor and for the shape of the conversion curve, but it should
  not be treated as empirically validated throughput. Recommend damping the
  aggregate multiplier in playtest rather than trusting the source's own math.
- **Zero clean primary-source numbers exist for "coherence" as a standalone,
  measured quantity.** Every source that discusses coherence discusses it
  qualitatively (stereotyped writing, dogmatism, "common language on basic
  theoretical problems,"
  `reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-4/mswv4_59.htm`) or via a proxy
  (pre-revolutionary cadre fraction, achievement-threshold). No source gives a
  direct "coherence points per study-hour" figure. This term of the TL formula
  is therefore the least evidence-grounded and should be treated as a
  playtest-calibrated multiplier from day one, not derived from history.
- **Design-law tension: "no optimal path" vs. real convergence data.**
  Several findings show a real historical current (Bolshevik "Pravdism")
  converging on ~79% dominance of its field within 2.5 years
  (Lenin, `prolewiki/Exports/Library/Vladimir Lenin_Adventurism.txt`) — i.e.
  a real trunk *can* look locally optimal for a stretch. The doctrine tree's
  "no optimal path" law should therefore be read as a claim about *long-run,
  cross-era* stability, not about short-run dominance; a trunk should be
  able to win decisively for years before its trap catches up, not be
  permanently balanced tick-to-tick.
- **Press-spend split (2.76:1 mass:internal) is one snapshot (Nov 1920,
  one small party) and may not generalize** to a mature, legal-era press
  operation, where the *Guardian*/`Call` data (§1.3) shows internal cadre
  papers and mass papers converging into single weekly organs rather than
  staying split. Recommend treating the 2.76:1 ratio as an *early-underground*
  default only, with a design hook to shift the split as an organization
  matures — history documents both patterns but at different organizational
  ages, not as competing claims about the same stage.
