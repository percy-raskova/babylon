# Design Brief: Infiltration & Surveillance
### Grounding the Desert falsification state, the Ambush Trap, and state-side vision (Epistemic Horizon mechanic)

## 0. Scope and method

This brief synthesizes primary-source and encyclopedia-summary evidence on state infiltration,
informants, agent provocateurs, and deception in class struggle, for the Epistemic Horizon (EH)
mechanic. Citations are local file paths from the corpus. Secondary compilations (prolewiki
exports, `subject/stalinism/*` overview chapters) are flagged explicitly and weighted lower for
numeric derivation, higher only for narrative/pattern confirmation, versus primary documents (DoJ
memos, party bulletins, Comintern theses, Lenin/Mao texts).

## 1. Historical evidence

### 1.1 The State sees everywhere, understands nowhere — the structural thesis

The clearest primary-source statement of the EH thesis comes from a side-by-side military
comparison: **"In the White areas the mere leakage of a piece of information may cause defeat in a
subsequent engagement, but in the Red areas such leakage is often not a very serious matter"**
(Mao, `reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-1/mswv1_12.htm`). Identical information carries
opposite strategic weight depending on whether the surrounding population is bound by relationship
— the exact asymmetry EH is built to model.

The U.S. state's own self-report confirms the pattern at scale. A. Mitchell Palmer's November 1919
Senate Report on the Radical Division program: **"a more or less complete history of over 60,000
radically-inclined individuals has been gathered together and classified,"** with 471 monitored
publications and 40 translators (`government/ussenate/1114-palmer-reporttosenate.pdf`), yet the
same Attorney General personally judged a fully-translated, fully-documented case (El Ariete, 1919)
legally inert: "I do not believe that the acts and deeds set forth in the indictment... establish
an offense" (same source). Presence of data ≠ comprehension. Palmer's public claim of omniscience
— **"the Department of Justice... is today a human net that no outlaw can escape"**
(`government/fbi/1920/0200-palmer-redscase.pdf`) — rested on ~500 agents nationwide covering an
alleged 60,000 radicals (~1:120 nominal coverage). The Jan 2, 1920 Palmer Raids ran off a single
coded telegram to 33 field offices firing on one generic script
(`government/fbi/1920/0102b-hoover-toboioffices2.pdf`) — bureaucratically omnipresent, locally
blind. A Philadelphia BoI informant memo (Sept. 1921) shows coverage was structurally uneven:
**"In Philadelphia alone, we have more than 28 different organizations affiliated with the
Communist Party... and with but one paid confidential informant, who only speaks the English
language"** (`government/fbi/1921/0920-foster-tohoover.pdf`) — ~1:28 informant-to-organization,
with an explicit language blind spot the Bureau itself flagged. Hoover's reply confirms HUMINT
targeted leadership, not the base: "we made an effort to have an informant in every one of the
leading movements... particularly those of a foreign nature" (same source) — surveillance is
hub-biased, so the mass base stays structurally the darkest zone even in "covered" organizations.

The 1930 CPUSA Language Bureau report gives a numbered Desert zone at national scale:
**"Organizations consisting of over a million members... have no Party members and no
influence"** (`parties/cpusa/1930/0600-cpusa-languagework.pdf`), alongside a real cadre-leverage
ratio — controlled orgs run at roughly 1 cadre per 10 masses (5,000/50,000), merely-influenced
orgs at roughly 1 per 175 (800/140,000). The same report names the Mud state's failure mode: cadre
"develop a tendency of giving to these organizations almost the role of the Party" — an
echo-chamber bias where captured small cells are mistaken for genuine mass sentiment.

### 1.2 Informants, agent provocateurs, and their real tenures

DoJ's own 1920 memo on provocateurs preceding the Palmer Raids instructs field offices: **"If
possible, you should arrange with your undercover informants to have meetings of the Communist
Party... held on the night set"** (`dept-justice/1920/0524-doj-provocateurs.pdf`) — the informant
does not merely observe, he *engineers the object being surveilled*. The same file documents Herman
Bernhard inserted into the Buffalo CP local **20 days** after the raids (rising to become recording
secretary), and Ferdinand "Nosovitsky" Petersen, a well-placed informant whose "work... was not
entirely satisfactory... he withdrew from the service in November 1919" after roughly **three
months** — even good access can yield a short, low-value tenure. On his way out, Petersen seeded a
false accusation that Louis Fraina was a DoJ agent, triggering a real internal "Party Trial" that
consumed cadre time on a witch hunt of an innocent leader — a parting act of pure disinformation,
not information loss.

Tenure varies wildly: a Stanford student walk-in informer on the Bay Area Revolutionary Union
lasted **about one week** before "retiring," never past attendance-list intelligence
(`erol/ncm-1/ru-fbi.htm`) — blown by the RU's own social/mentor network, not counter-surveillance
tradecraft. William J. Burns ran embedded operatives inside the Iron Workers for **five to six
years (1905–1911)**, culminating in the McNamara conviction, a decade of subsequent labor-movement
paralysis, and a dictagraph wiretap penetrating even privileged attorney-client conversation
(`government/fbi/1921/0818-nycall-burnsdirector.pdf`). William O'Neal's infiltration of the Chicago
BPP ran roughly **five years (1968–1973)**, paid at escalating rates (~$100/week to $300/month),
rising to chief of security, delivering a floor plan that enabled two raids six months apart, the
second fatal (Fred Hampton, Dec 4 1969) — `prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_The Assassination of
Fred Hampton.txt`. N.N. Mikhailov, a dentist with legitimate social access, rolled up an entire
founding St. Petersburg circle in one night raid (Dec 8, 1895) — `archive/lenin/works/1901/witbd/ch02.htm`.

### 1.3 Falsification is active, not passive — the Desert design's core claim

Repeatedly, deception manufactures false-but-normal-looking data, not merely hidden data. During
the Kronstadt siege, a **fabricated troop-movement rumor**, "put out by the Communists in the hope
of breaking up the Conference," dispersed a ~300-delegate meeting in alarm
(`history/ussr/events/kronstadt/izvestia/09.htm`); Petrogradskaia Pravda ran invented "White Guard
officer" battle narratives Kronstadt's own bulletin had to rebut point-by-point (`izvestia/12.htm`).
Batista's regime after the Moncada attack reported a coherent, format-correct, wholly invented
casualty account — zero wounded, ever — that "looked normal" right up to contradiction by survivors
(`history/cuba/archive/castro/1953/10/16.htm`). The Chicago Tribune, with police cooperation,
fabricated an entire "Michigan manhunt" narrative for William Z. Foster while he sat in a courthouse
conference across town (`unions/tuel/1922/0826-haessler-tribune.pdf`). FBI COINTELPRO forged letters
to split the Newton and Cleaver factions of the Black Panther Party by accusing each side of police
collusion (`prolewiki/Exports/Main/COINTELPRO.txt`) — an attack on the SOLIDARITY edge itself, not a
data-extraction operation. Falsification is also bidirectional: the CPA and CLP both padded their
own membership reports to Moscow (`parties/cpusa/1921/0713-grimes-boimemo.pdf`); the KPD claimed
"one million workers on strike" in the 1921 March Action when the true figure was 200,000–300,000
(`subject/germany-1918-23/dauve-authier/ch15.htm`); Roger Baldwin publicly exposed a Comintern
cutout identity, "Charles Edward Scott," representing a nonexistent "Pan-American Agency"
(`unions/iww/1921/0429-leader-baldwin.pdf`) — revolutionary organizations manufacture Mud for
outside observers too.

### 1.4 Self-inflicted Desert: paranoia, witch hunts, and purges

The single most important corrective finding: **falsified infiltration-fear is often more
destructive than real infiltration.** The Minsaengdan Incident in the Jiandao guerrilla bases
(1932–1935) is the clearest case: Japanese intelligence tried and failed to plant ten-man
infiltration cells (all captured, 0% success against a high-cohesion base —
`prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Reminscences...Volume 4.txt`), so it pivoted to seeding a rumor
of mass infiltration instead. **7–8 actual infiltrators triggered a purge that executed over 2,000
of the base's own cadre over roughly three years** — a ~250x overkill ratio, corrected only when
top leadership intervened. The 1938 dissolution of the Communist Party of Poland ran on an
*unproven, never-substantiated* Comintern infiltration accusation, after which 30 of 37 1932
Central Committee members were shot or imprisoned (`erol/ncm-6/costello-poland.htm`, a
secondary/encyclopedia source). PRRWO's 1976 internal "bourgeois plot" purge used torture-extracted
confessions to destroy at least three-quarters of its own membership (`erol/ncm-3/wing-history.htm`);
a companion pamphlet concluded PRRWO's own general secretary might have been the provocateur, but
the source preserves this as unresolved. József Pogány was shot in the 1937–38 Great Purge on a
fabricated "Hungarian spy" charge (`parties/cpusa/1940/0603-ballam-johnsoninterview.pdf`). Mao names
the cure in a Dec. 1955 corrective speech: **"If you do not do so and suspect everybody, in the end
you will find yourselves suspicious of your own shadow"** and sets a *tolerated* bad-actor floor:
"even though there may be a bad one in 28, you will still have 27 good people"
(`selected-works/volume-9/mswv9_38.htm`) — a ~3.6% baseline informant density a mass base is
expected to absorb without a witch hunt.

### 1.5 Rollup and reconstitution timescales

The CPUSA's own 1920 experience (M. Golos oral history, `parties/cpusa/1940/0000-mgolos-johnsoninterview.pdf`):
the Jan. 2, 1920 Palmer Raids netted 600–700 of a ~12,000-member Federation (~5–10% seizure), yet
an illegal apparatus was reconstituted by **March 1920 (~2 months)**, though "it was very hard to
see people at that time." A seized print shop was rebuilt in **two weeks**. Party founding to mass
arrest ran **three months** (Sept. 1919 CP/CLP founding → Jan. 1920 raids, 5,000–6,000 arrested —
`parties/cpusa/1940/0603-ballam-johnsoninterview.pdf`); the 1978-founded CPUSA/ML was split by an
infiltrator faction after **nine months** (`erol/ncm-7/illegal-party.htm`) — bracketing a
"young-organization vulnerability window" of 3–9 months. UCP membership rebuilt to a comparable
scale within **eleven months** of the arrests. Seized-document processing lagged **7–10 days**
behind a raid (`government/fbi/1921/0501-scully-casesummary.pdf`), while tip-off to raid execution
ran **3–4 days** — intelligence from a raid should not resolve instantly.

### 1.6 Mass receptivity: growth, thresholds, and collapse

Growth can be organic and slow (IWW's Agricultural Workers Organization: 0 to 8,000+ in ~18 months
of pure field-delegate organizing, no repression yet — `unions/iww/1930/25years.htm`) or
explosively threshold-triggered (Hunan peasant associations: ~5x growth in about four months once
fear-of-reprisal lifted — `mswv1_2.htm`; agricultural cooperatives: ~4x in ~5 months, 1955 —
`mswv5_47.htm`). Under acute crisis it can be near-instant: 66,000 Petrograd garrison troops
flipped allegiance in under 24 hours (Feb 1917, `history/ussr/events/timeline/1917.htm`); the
German November Revolution spread from one naval mutiny to ten-plus major cities including Berlin
in **six days** (`subject/germany-1918-23/sewell/chapter2.htm`). Collapse can be equally fast:
Kronstadt Izvestia records over 130 individually-named defections within roughly **nine days**
(`izvestia/08.htm`); the SPD lost 80% of its membership over the roughly three years of WWI
betrayal (`ch04.htm`). Receptivity is not merely additive to repression: IWW/AWIU membership "more
than doubled" in 1917 and *continued rising through* the Sept. 1917 nationwide anti-IWW raids
before delayed effects set in later (`unions/iww/1930/25years.htm`) — prior relationship-capital
buys a lag against shock decay.

### 1.7 Cell structure as a security-versus-reach tradeoff

The CPUSA's 1935 Organizer's Manual sets a hard numeric security cap: **"The Shop Unit which grows
to over 10-12 members should be divided into two independent working groups as quickly as
possible"** (`parties/cpusa/1935/07/organisers-manual/ch03.htm`), and states the Water-thesis almost
verbatim: "the Shop Unit will remain the most solid link with the masses under any conditions
(terror, illegality)." The Profintern's 1929 Strasbourg conference gives a matching
representation-density formula — 1 committee member per 25–50 workers in small shops, 1 per
100–200 in large ones (`history/international/profintern/strike_strategy/ch05.htm`) — plus a
recallability clause functioning as a real-time anti-informant mechanism, and doctrine for
surviving repression via redundant, distributed leadership so no single arrest wave decapitates
the network (`ch16.htm`).

## 2. Design recommendations

**Tick-length assumption.** Several primary sources converge on a **weekly** cadence for routine
mass-line reporting (Comintern Third Congress, "All members must report approximately once a week"
— `comintern/3rd-congress/party-theses.htm`; IWW job-branch bulletins weekly — `unions/iww/1921/kennedy_how.htm`;
League of Revolutionary Struggle national bulletins every 4–5 days — `erol/ncm-5/lrs-postal-2.htm`).
**Recommendation: treat 1 game tick ≈ 1 week** for all EH cadence coefficients below unless noted.
This is an assumption inherited from the historical cadence, not a verified engine constant —
confirm against the actual `SimulationConfig`/tick-length convention before hard-coding.

### 2.4 Event/action designs

- **Ambush event (state-side).** Grounded in Oskar Hippe's 1923 Mücheln ambush — plausible social
  contact ("asked the way to the station... did not seem suspicious") converts to violent seizure
  (`subject/germany-1918-23/hippe/ch04.htm`) and Mikhailov's dentist-cover infiltration
  (`witbd/ch02.htm`). An ESTABLISH_CONTACT_NETWORK action in a Desert-masked node should carry a
  hidden chance to resolve as a state ambush rather than a genuine contact — a normal-looking
  success message that is actually the trap closing. No numeric trigger rate exists in the corpus;
  **flag for playtest calibration**, seeded low (ambush setup should cost the State AI real
  resources, per Burns's multi-year Iron Workers operation).
- **Falsified-normal event (Desert masking).** A Desert reading should occasionally substitute a
  *plausible false narrative* rather than blank data — per the fabricated Kronstadt troop rumor
  (`izvestia/09.htm`), the Batista casualty report (`castro/1953/10/16.htm`), and the Chicago
  Tribune manhunt (`tuel/1922/0826-haessler-tribune.pdf`). This is the mechanical difference between
  Desert and mere "no data": full internal consistency and format-normalcy.
- **Self-inflicted witch-hunt trap.** Triggerable when perceived (not actual) State I_c pressure
  exceeds a threshold while internal `coherence` is low — grounded in Minsaengdan, the 1938 Polish
  CP dissolution, and PRRWO 1976. Cross-cutting across all four trunks, not trunk-specific. A Party
  Congress action landing mid-witch-hunt should cut the overkill ratio, per Kim Il Sung's 1955
  corrective intervention.
- **Congress-effectiveness maturation curve.** The 1919-22 CP/CLP schism took **~36 months** to
  resolve via successive non-binding Comintern rulings; the 1928-29 Bolshevization correction took
  **~5 months** (`parties/cpusa/1940/0000-dirba-johnsoninterview.pdf`). Congress correction-speed
  should scale with accumulated institutional maturity rather than a fixed cadence — young Party
  Congresses slow and partial, mature ones fast.
- **Bidirectional falsification.** Desert-masking should be available to BOTH sides: State-on-
  Revolution (default case above), Revolution-on-observer (Baldwin/Comintern cutout identity,
  `iww/1921/0429-leader-baldwin.pdf`), faction-on-Moscow (CPA/CLP membership padding,
  `parties/cpusa/1921/0713-grimes-boimemo.pdf`), and faction-on-faction (Bill Dunne's phony DoJ
  badges, `parties/cpusa/1940/0000-mgolos-johnsoninterview.pdf`).

## 3. Tensions and open questions

- **The foco exception.** Castro's Moncada assault (`castro/1953/10/16.htm`) is the one strong case
  where a small, secretive cadre (explicitly *not* mass-line-built) staged a dramatic action and
  only afterward expected receptivity to follow via broadcast revolutionary laws. If "you cannot
  SCOUT your way to intelligence" is a universal design law, this is the counter-example it is built
  to exclude — flag in-engine as illegitimate/unstable, not a quiet alternate path to Water.
- **PRRWO's cause of collapse stays genuinely unknowable.** Ex-cadre could never conclusively
  determine whether their general secretary was an agent-provocateur or an organic ultra-left
  failure (`erol/ncm-3/wing-history.htm`). EH should preserve this ambiguity for some trap
  outcomes — not every Desert trigger should resolve to a clean infiltrated-yes/no flag.
- **Repression saturates rather than compounds linearly.** Lenin observed that after ~20 years of
  tsarist emergency law, "the population is gradually becoming inured... and is ceasing to react to
  fresh injections of it" (`archive/lenin/works/1902/feb/15.htm`) — yet the SPD/Kronstadt cases show
  repression/betrayal producing *sharp* short-term crashes. Recommend distinct short-horizon
  (sharp) and long-horizon (saturating) decay terms rather than one formula.
- **Theoretical Labor overhead is contested.** Mao attacks over-investment in study at the expense
  of cadre-presence (40 days of document study observed vs. his recommended ~1 day — `mswv9_38.htm`),
  while Cannon's 1924 registration campaign argues a full month of internal self-knowledge-gathering
  is a legitimate prerequisite (`parties/cpusa/1924/0725-cannon-industrialregist.pdf`). Not strictly
  contradictory, but they pull differently on tolerable TL overhead; needs playtest tuning.
- **No hard number exists** for ambush-trigger probability, hub-node I_c multiplier, or
  repression-decay lag — named above as playtest-calibration items, not history-derived constants.
- **Coverage gap:** this sweep is heavy on 1917-1980 US/Russia/China cases and light on
  non-Comintern-aligned insurgencies (Irish, anti-colonial African cases appear only thinly);
  Program-20/Spectrum-of-Unequal-Exchange material may hold complementary data not surfaced here.
