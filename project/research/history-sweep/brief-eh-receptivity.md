# Design Brief: EH-Receptivity — Timing the Mass Receptivity (M_r) Curve Against the Weekly Tick

**Theme:** when and how fast masses open and close to agitation, revolutionary situations, dual power, and post-defeat decay.
**Mechanic:** Epistemic Horizon (M_r, I_c, Desert/Mud/Water). Assumed tick length: **1 week** (matches the game's tick cadence and, independently, several primary-source organizational reporting cycles found below).

---

## 1. Historical Evidence

### 1.1 Fast receptivity spikes (crisis-triggered, sub-tick to few-tick timescale)

The clearest pattern in the corpus is that M_r does not rise smoothly under acute crisis — it **phase-changes** in days.

- The Petrograd garrison flip, 27 Feb 1917: "By nightfall, 66,000 men of the Petrograd garrison — a day ago ordered to fire on striking workers — have now joined the striking workers, fully armed" (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/ussr/events/timeline/1917.htm`). An entire armed institution flipped allegiance in under 24 hours.
- The Kornilov affair, 27–30 Aug 1917: agitators dissolved an elite loyalist division (including the "Savage Division") from within in about three days (same source).
- The Kiel mutiny to nationwide council power, Nov 1918: Kiel (3 Nov) → Hamburg/Bremen/Lübeck (6 Nov) → eight more major cities (7–8 Nov) → Berlin (9 Nov) — a full-country receptivity spike in six days (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/subject/germany-1918-23/sewell/chapter2.htm`). ~10,000 workers'/soldiers' councils were spontaneously established within days (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/subject/germany-1918-23/dauve-authier/ch05.htm`) — but note (§3) that this undirected spike defaulted to the pre-existing SPD apparatus, not the revolutionary vanguard, because the KPD/Spartacists had "no one on the executive of the councils in Berlin" despite a national KPD founding congress days later (3,000 cadre against millions mobilized) — cadre-presence is a genuinely separate variable from raw mass mood.
- German strike-wave escalation, 1916–1918, each wave larger than the last on a specific trigger: ~50,000 (May 1916, Liebknecht's arrest) → ~250,000 (Apr 1917, Berlin) → 1,000,000+ (Jan 1918, Brest-Litovsk) (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/subject/germany-1918-23/dauve-authier/ch04.htm`).
- Hunan peasant-association membership, Oct 1926–Jan 1927: flat for ~9 months of organizing, then "jumped to two million" (~5x) in roughly four months once the Northern Expedition removed fear of landlord reprisal (`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-1/mswv1_2.htm`). A second, independent data point for the same ~4–5x phase-change multiplier: agricultural-cooperative membership went from 16.9 million to over 70 million households in about five months in 1955 (`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-5/mswv5_47.htm`).
- 1922 Shopmen's Strike solidarity, per Foster: mass solidarity surged in days across the entire national railroad workforce, then collapsed the instant several union leaderships betrayed the base (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/unions/tuel/1922/1000-foster-shopmensstrike.pdf`) — a distinct "leadership-betrayal" decay event, separate from ordinary decay.

Counter-evidence that receptivity is **not always** this fast: Lenin explicitly distinguishes a slow-changing ideological baseline ("revolutionary defencism," patriotic conditioning) from acute crisis spikes — shifting deep ideological alignment is "not a very rapid success, but one that will be real and enduring" (`archive/lenin/works/1917/tasks/ch05.htm`). AWO/AWIU grew from 0 to over 8,000 members in ~18 months of pure field-delegate organizing with no crisis trigger (`history/usa/unions/iww/1930/25years.htm`) — a much slower, additive baseline growth curve. Anyuan Colliery: spare-time schools → formal union (May 1922) → mass strike (Sept 1922, won in 5 days) is a ~4-month organic build (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-9/mswv9_38.htm`).

### 1.2 Fast receptivity collapse (rupture / betrayal-triggered)

- Kronstadt, March 1921: the garrison's own bulletin documents "declarations of departure from the R.C.P. arrive unceasingly," with one issue alone listing 130+ named defections within roughly 9 days of the uprising's start (`history/ussr/events/kronstadt/izvestia/08.htm`), and casualties/armed clash documented by day 13 from the initial no-confidence resolution (`history/ussr/events/kronstadt/izvestia/12.htm`). Crucially, the stated cause is explicitly relational: "finding the party ... completely bureaucratized and absolutely separated from the masses, we are leaving its ranks" (`history/ussr/events/kronstadt/izvestia/08.htm`) — the collapse is framed as broken relationship, not raw repression.
- SPD membership collapse under wartime betrayal: 1,000,000 (1914) → 200,000 (Sept 1917), an 80% defection over ~3 years (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/subject/germany-1918-23/dauve-authier/ch04.htm`) — a slower, cumulative version of the same betrayal-driven mechanism.
- CPA (Australia) 1949 Coal Strike: a continuation vote flipped from 90% in favor (7,995–882) to ~75% against (6,974–2,378) in roughly two months once the rank and file perceived cadre withholding information (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/subject/stalinism/into-mainstream/ch03.htm`).
- Counter-example: AWO/AWIU membership "more than doubled" during 1917 and **continued rising through** the Sept. 5, 1917 nationwide IWW-hall raids, before delayed effects set in months later (`history/usa/unions/iww/1930/25years.htm`). Repression alone, absent a felt betrayal of relationship, does not reliably crash M_r — this is a real tension with the Kronstadt/CPA cases (see §3).

### 1.3 Repression, rollup, and reconstitution timescales

- Palmer Raids, Jan 2 1920: 5,000–6,000 arrested nationwide, three months after the CP/CLP's founding (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1940/0603-ballam-johnsoninterview.pdf`) — a hard "young-org vulnerability window" of ~3 months.
- Post-raid recovery, per M. Golos and Charles Dirba oral histories: underground apparatus reconstituted in ~2 months (Jan→March 1920), though "it was very hard to see people at that time" (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1940/0000-mgolos-johnsoninterview.pdf`); full underground operation ran ~23 months (Jan 1920–Dec 1921) before the first re-legalization attempt (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1940/0000-dirba-johnsoninterview.pdf`); UCP national membership recovered to a comparable scale (5,801 across 12 districts) within ~11 months of the 5,000–6,000 arrests (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1921/0102-ucp-conventionminutes.pdf`). Propaganda infrastructure specifically (a seized print shop) rebuilt in ~2 weeks — much faster than the full underground apparatus (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1940/0000-mgolos-johnsoninterview.pdf`).
- Agent reinsertion after a raid: DoJ special agent Herman Bernhard was inserted into the Buffalo CP local 20 days after the Jan 2 raid (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/government/dept-justice/1920/0524-doj-provocateurs.pdf`).
- Longer-horizon rebuilds: the Bulgarian CP, banned after the 1923 insurrection, took roughly a decade to exceed its pre-suppression strength (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Georgi Dimitrov_ Selected Speeches and Articles.txt`); Dimitrov and Kolarov's own exile after that defeat lasted 22 years (same source). CPA Australia lost half its membership (12,000→6,000) over ~2–3 years of sustained-but-legal state harassment (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/subject/stalinism/into-mainstream/ch04.htm`) — a slower attrition curve than acute rupture, useful as a "hostile-but-legal" decay baseline.
- Minsaengdan self-purge, Jiandao guerrilla bases, 1932–35: a Japanese-manufactured infiltration panic drove the movement to execute ~2,000 of its own cadre against 7–8 actual infiltrators over roughly three years, before Kim Il Sung's leadership intervened; assessment at the time was that unchecked purging would collapse the entire guerrilla zone within 1–2 years (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_Reminscences With the Century_ The Anti-Japanese Revolution Volume 3 & 4.txt`). This is the strongest documented case of **self-inflicted Desert-state collapse** exceeding any external threat.

### 1.4 Cadre-density, cadre:mass ratios, and organizational architecture

- CPUSA Language Bureau report, 1930: fully controlled organizations ran at ~1 cadre per 10 masses (5,000/50,000); merely "influenced" organizations at ~1:175 (800/140,000); and — in the same report, at the same historical moment — "organizations consisting of over a million members ... have no Party members and no influence," an explicit, numbered zero-cadre Desert zone (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1930/0600-cpusa-languagework.pdf`).
- Philadelphia, 1921: the Bureau of Investigation had just 1 confidential informant (monolingual) covering 28+ Communist-affiliated organizations (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/government/fbi/1921/0920-foster-tohoover.pdf`); Hoover's reply confirms state HUMINT targets leadership/hub nodes, not the rank-and-file (same source).
- CPUSA Organizer's Manual (1935): a factory Shop Unit exceeding 10–12 members must split immediately, trading reach for security (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1935/07/organisers-manual/ch03.htm`); the same manual states neighborhood papers at 200–300 readers each, multiplied across many units, were calculated to yield close to a million-worker relationship coverage.
- Comintern Third Congress theses (1921): "All members must report approximately once a week to the cell" (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/international/comintern/3rd-congress/party-theses.htm`); IWW Industrial Pioneer (1921) describes an identical weekly job-branch bulletin cadence feeding an industrial-union-wide information loop (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/unions/iww/1921/kennedy_how.htm`). Both independently land on a **weekly** cadence for the basic mass-line reporting loop.
- Mao, 1957 and 1965: "we mean 90 to 98 per cent" of the population as reachable/recoverable mass base (`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-5/mswv5_68.htm`); elsewhere, "even though there may be a bad one in 28, you will still have 27 good people" (~3.6% tolerated informant/bad-actor density) (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-9/mswv9_38.htm`).
- Hunan class survey, 1927: poor peasants 70%, middle peasants 20%, landlords/rich peasants 10% of the rural population, with sharply different receptivity behavior per tier — poor organized instantly and led, middle "waited and saw," rich resisted until coerced (`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-1/mswv1_2.htm`).

### 1.5 Infiltration, informants, and the Desert vision-state

Primary-source DoJ/FBI case files (not encyclopedia summaries) document the mechanics of state infiltration in unusual detail:

- The Jan. 2, 1920 nationwide raid instructions explicitly told field agents to "arrange with your undercover informants to have meetings ... held on the night set" — the state *engineers* the object it surveils, not merely observes it (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/government/dept-justice/1920/0524-doj-provocateurs.pdf`).
- Informant quality varies wildly and is often low: Ferdinand Petersen's DoJ tenure against Soviet Bureau contacts lasted Sept–Nov 1919 (~3 months) and was judged low-yield before he withdrew (same source); his parting act was to seed a false accusation against Louis Fraina that triggered a secret internal Party "trial" — a real historical cost of Desert-falsification landing as internal paranoia, not just lost visibility.
- A Stanford student's walk-in FBI stint against the Bay Area Revolutionary Union lasted about one week and never advanced past attendance-taking at meetings before the RU's own social network (a professor-student trust relationship) identified and neutralized him (`history/erol/ncm-1/ru-fbi.htm`) — real relationship-based counter-intelligence outperformed the state's dossier apparatus, which nonetheless held instant, complete personal data (SSN, draft number, address) on the target.
- By contrast, William O'Neal's infiltration of the Chicago Black Panther Party ran ~5 years (1968–1973), rose to chief of security, supplied the floor plan behind both the June and the fatal Dec. 4, 1969 raids, and simultaneously pushed the chapter toward more militaristic tactics (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/prolewiki/Exports/Library/Library_The Assassination of Fred Hampton.txt`) — a single embedded, high-commitment informant compromised both intelligence and doctrine over a multi-year horizon.
- Falsification is bidirectional and inter-factional, not only State-vs-Revolution: rival CP/UCP factions doubled memberships in reports to Moscow (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/parties/cpusa/1921/0713-grimes-boimemo.pdf`); Chicago Tribune fabricated an entire "Michigan manhunt" narrative for William Z. Foster while he sat in a courthouse conference (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/usa/unions/tuel/1922/0826-haessler-tribune.pdf`); a fabricated troop-movement rumor dispersed ~300 delegates at the Kronstadt Conference (`history/ussr/events/kronstadt/izvestia/09.htm`); COINTELPRO forged letters split the Newton/Cleaver factions of the Black Panther Party in March 1971 (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/prolewiki/Exports/Main/COINTELPRO.txt`).
- The clearest primary-source statement of the entire EH thesis: "In the White areas the mere leakage of a piece of information may cause defeat ... but in the Red areas such leakage is often not a very serious matter" (Mao, `reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-1/mswv1_12.htm`) — identical information has opposite strategic value depending on whether the surrounding population is bound by relationship.

## 2. Design Recommendations

All coefficients below convert historical durations to a **1 tick = 1 week** basis. Where history gives no usable number, that is stated explicitly and the value is flagged `NEEDS PLAYTEST`.

### 2.1 M_r decay rates

| Coefficient | Historical derivation | Recommended starting value |
|---|---|---|
| `RUPTURE_DECAY_RATE` (per tick, multiplicative, once a betrayal/rupture trigger fires) | Kronstadt: mass defection cascade visible within 9 days (1.3 ticks), full collapse to armed conflict by day 13 (1.9 ticks) from a Water-level starting relationship (`history/ussr/events/kronstadt/izvestia/08.htm`, `/12.htm`). Solving `M_r0·(1-k)^2 ≈ 0.05·M_r0` over 2 ticks gives k≈0.75. | **0.70–0.75/tick**, applied only 1–2 ticks after a rupture trigger (leadership betrayal, felt "separation from the masses") — NOT a generic repression response. |
| `BASELINE_ATTRITION_RATE` (per tick, no reinforcing mass-line action, sustained-but-legal repression) | CPA Australia: 12,000→6,000 members over ~2–3 years (110–150 weeks) of legal harassment (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/subject/stalinism/into-mainstream/ch04.htm`). `ln(0.5)/130 ≈ -0.0053`. | **~0.5%/tick** decay floor absent any AGITATE_LOCALLY/SOCIAL_INVESTIGATION/ESTABLISH_CONTACT_NETWORK action in a territory. |
| `LONG_HORIZON_EROSION_RATE` (per tick, absent active mass-line investment, no acute repression) | Polish United Workers' Party working-class share: 61%→45% over 6 years (~313 weeks) (`history/erol/ncm-6/costello-poland.htm`). `ln(0.7377)/313 ≈ -0.00097`. | **~0.1%/tick** — a slower, structural baseline distinct from the repression-driven rate above; use for organizational composition drift, not raw M_r. |

### 2.2 M_r growth rates

| Coefficient | Historical derivation | Recommended starting value |
|---|---|---|
| `PHASE_CHANGE_GROWTH_MULTIPLIER` (applied only once a threshold material/fear condition is met, e.g. dispossession relieved, protector force withdrawn) | Hunan peasant associations: ~5x in ~17 weeks (`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-1/mswv1_2.htm`); agricultural co-ops: ~4x in ~22 weeks (`reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-5/mswv5_47.htm`). Geometric mean ≈ 4.5x over ~19.5 weeks → per-tick multiplier `4.5^(1/19.5) ≈ 1.078`. | **~1.07–1.08/tick** compounding, active only during a bounded "mobilization window" (recommend 15–25 ticks) after threshold conditions are met — not a permanent multiplier. |
| `BASELINE_ORGANIC_GROWTH_RATE` (steady field organizing, no crisis trigger) | AWO/AWIU: 0→8,000+ members in ~18 months (~78 weeks) (`history/usa/unions/iww/1930/25years.htm`). Linear/logistic approximation gives an early-phase per-tick addition rate on the order of `8000/78 ≈ 103 members/tick` for a single branch — a scale reference, not a universal rate. | **NEEDS PLAYTEST** for translation into an abstract M_r units/tick figure; use only as a relative-scale sanity check (baseline growth is roughly an order of magnitude slower than phase-change growth). |
| `MASS_LINE_ACTION_CADENCE` | Comintern 3rd Congress theses: weekly cell reporting (`/media/user/data/old-hdd/old-hdd/www.marxists.org/history/international/comintern/3rd-congress/party-theses.htm`); IWW weekly job-branch bulletins (`history/usa/unions/iww/1921/kennedy_how.htm`). | Confirms **1 tick = 1 mass-line-action opportunity per cadre-node** is historically grounded, not an arbitrary game abstraction. |
| `AGITATE_CRITICAL_SUCCESS_MULTIPLIER` | IWW General Defense Committee: "With Drops of Blood" pamphlet yielded $22,000+ in its first month vs. a prior $9,000 baseline, a ~2.4x jump (`history/usa/unions/iww/1930/25years.htm`). | **~2–2.5x** one-tick spike on top of base AGITATE_LOCALLY yield for a well-crafted narrative artifact (rare, not the default roll). |

### 2.3 Repression / infiltration timing

| Coefficient | Historical derivation | Recommended starting value |
|---|---|---|
| `YOUNG_ORG_VULNERABILITY_WINDOW` | Palmer Raids struck 3 months (13 weeks) after CP/CLP founding (`.../1940/0603-ballam-johnsoninterview.pdf`); CPUSA/ML split by an infiltrator faction 9 months (39 weeks) after its Dec. 1978 founding (`history/erol/ncm-7/illegal-party.htm`). | **13–39 ticks** elevated infiltration/rollup risk for any newly-founded organization node; taper after ~40 ticks. |
| `UNDERGROUND_RECONSTITUTION_TIME` (after a mass-raid Desert-purge) | ~2 months (8–9 weeks) to a functioning but hard-to-contact underground apparatus (`.../1940/0000-mgolos-johnsoninterview.pdf`); ~11 months (47 weeks) to full pre-raid membership parity (`.../1921/0102-ucp-conventionminutes.pdf`). | **8–9 ticks** to restore minimal I_c/contact function; **~47 ticks** to restore full pre-shock M_r. Two-stage recovery curve, not one constant. |
| `PROPAGANDA_CHANNEL_REBUILD_TIME` | Seized Russian-language print shop reorganized in 2 weeks (`.../1940/0000-mgolos-johnsoninterview.pdf`). | **2 ticks** — propaganda/communication-channel infrastructure recovers far faster than the full contact network; model as a separate, faster-recovering sub-resource. |
| `STATE_REINSERTION_LAG` (new informant after a purge/raid) | Buffalo local: replacement DoJ agent inserted 20 days (~3 ticks) after the Jan 2 1920 raid (`.../1920/0524-doj-provocateurs.pdf`). | **3 ticks** before the State's Intel Confidence in a purged node begins rebuilding. |
| `INTEL_PROCESSING_LAG` (raid → usable intelligence) | Lindgren case: "at least a week or ten days" to process seized documents (`.../1921/0501-scully-casesummary.pdf`). | **1–2 ticks** delay between a successful raid/seizure and the resulting I_c gain becoming available to the seizing side. |
| `CELL_SIZE_CAP` | CPUSA Organizer's Manual: Shop Units split above 10–12 members (`.../1935/07/organisers-manual/ch03.htm`). | **10–12** cadre per contact-network node before mandatory split; exceeding it should raise per-node infiltration risk. |

### 2.4 Cadre-presence / Intel Confidence multipliers

| Coefficient | Historical derivation | Recommended starting value |
|---|---|---|
| `CADRE_TIER_MULTIPLIER` (controlled / influenced / desert) | CPUSA 1930: controlled ≈1:10 cadre:mass, influenced ≈1:175, desert = 0 cadre / >1,000,000 masses (`.../1930/0600-cpusa-languagework.pdf`). | Controlled tier → multiplier **1.0**; influenced tier → **0.15–0.2** (NEEDS PLAYTEST — the raw ratio 10/175≈0.057 undershoots the qualitative "real influence" the source describes, so start higher); desert tier → **0.0**. |
| `TOLERATED_INFORMANT_DENSITY` (noise floor below which a mass base stays net-positive) | Mao: "a bad one in 28" ≈ 3.6% (`.../mao/selected-works/volume-9/mswv9_38.htm`). | **~3.6%** informant/bad-actor density floor; below this, ESTABLISH_CONTACT_NETWORK actions should still net-positive M_r. |
| `MASS_RECEPTIVITY_CEILING` in a well-organized territory | Mao 1957: "90 to 98 per cent" reachable (`.../mao/selected-works/volume-5/mswv5_68.htm`). | **0.90–0.98** ceiling on M_r in a fully-organized territory, with a **2–10%** permanently-hostile floor. |
| `CLASS_FACTOR` lookup | Hunan 1927 survey: 70% poor / 20% middle / 10% landlord-rich, with markedly different receptivity behavior per tier (`.../mao/selected-works/volume-1/mswv1_2.htm`). | Population-share weights are historically grounded (70/20/10); the **per-tier multiplier values** (e.g., poor=1.0, middle=0.4, landlord=0.05) are **NEEDS PLAYTEST** — history gives behavior direction, not the multiplier magnitude. |
| `HUB_NODE_CAPTURE_BONUS` | Capturing UCP Treasurer Lindgren "instantly" converted years of state suspicion into confirmed intelligence (`.../1921/0713-grimes-boimemo.pdf`). | **NEEDS PLAYTEST** for magnitude — recommend a one-tick step-jump (not gradual) of roughly +0.3 to +0.5 absolute I_c for the compromising side when a logistics/treasurer/organizer-role cadre is captured; no historical number given, only the qualitative "instant" framing. |
| `PUBLIC_BASELINE_I_C_CAP` | DOJ 1920: ~500 agents nationwide vs. 60,000 catalogued "radicals" (~1:120), 471 monitored publications, yet failed even to secure a conviction in a fully-documented case (El Ariete) (`.../1919/1114-palmer-reporttosenate.pdf`). | **Cap public-baseline I_c at ~0.1–0.2** regardless of surveillance volume — dossier scale does not convert to actionable confidence without relationship. |

### 2.5 Event/action designs

- **Desert Injection (State action).** Fabricate a plausible-looking false signal (rumor, forged letter, relabeled category) that either freezes a pending mass action or severs a SOLIDARITY edge. Historical magnitude anchor: one fabricated troop-movement rumor dispersed an entire ~300-delegate Conference at Kronstadt (`history/ussr/events/kronstadt/izvestia/09.htm`). Recommend AoE scaled to "one tick, one meeting/node," not territory-wide, absent further evidence.
- **Self-Inflicted Purge (Revolution-side failure mode).** An internal infiltration panic (real or false) can trigger a witch-hunt that destroys far more M_r/cadre than any external threat — Minsaengdan (~2,000 executed vs. 7–8 real infiltrators) and PRRWO (purge eliminated ~3/4 of membership) are the two strongest cases. This should be modeled as a distinct trap-adjacent event, mechanically separate from ordinary Desert-state decay, triggered when a paranoia/witch-hunt threshold is crossed rather than by actual informant density.
- **Hub vs. Mass Node Asymmetry.** Compromising a logistics/treasurer/contact-list role should produce a step-jump in adversary I_c; compromising rank-and-file nodes should not. Grounded qualitatively (Lindgren case) but the actual multiplier is a playtest parameter.
- **Two-Tier Recovery Curve.** After a Desert-purge event, model recovery as (a) a fast propaganda-channel restoration (~2 ticks) feeding into (b) a slower full-contact-network restoration (~8–9 ticks to minimal function, ~47 ticks to full parity).

## 3. Tensions and Open Questions

1. **Repression sometimes accelerates M_r, sometimes crashes it — the discriminator is relational, not the raw repression magnitude.** AWO membership rose through the Sept. 1917 raids (`history/usa/unions/iww/1930/25years.htm`) while Kronstadt collapsed from an internal act of "separation from the masses" (`history/ussr/events/kronstadt/izvestia/08.htm`) and CPA Australia collapsed under legal-not-violent harassment (`.../into-mainstream/ch03.htm`). The design should key `RUPTURE_DECAY_RATE` to a **felt-betrayal** trigger (leadership caught lying, promises broken), not to `repression_intensity` directly — but the current mechanic spec doesn't yet name that trigger explicitly. This needs a ruling.
2. **Self-inflicted paranoia can outweigh real infiltration by two to three orders of magnitude** (Minsaengdan: ~2,000 executed vs. 7–8 actual agents). If the EH mechanic only models State→Revolution infiltration, it misses the historically larger threat: the Revolution's own witch-hunt response. Recommend a design decision on whether paranoia is a separate meter or a decay multiplier on M_r itself.
3. **The Desert/Mud/Water 0.2/0.8 thresholds are not directly derivable from any single historical number.** Mao's 90–98% "reachable" ceiling and ~3.6% "tolerated informant" floor (`.../mswv9_38.htm`, `.../mswv5_68.htm`) are compatible with a high Water threshold, but no source gives a clean 0.2 cutoff for Desert. The thresholds as ratified should be treated as game-design choices, only loosely bounded by history, not as derived constants.
4. **Causal ambiguity should possibly be a permanent design feature, not a bug.** Ex-PRRWO cadre could never establish whether their collapse was caused by a real agent (Fontañez) or organic sectarian dogmatism (`history/erol/ncm-3/wing-history.htm`) — the historical record itself never resolves it. If the game always reveals ground truth on a Trap's cause, that is *less honest* than the history it's modeling.
5. **Cadre-presence and mass mood can diverge sharply during the same crisis** — the Nov. 1918 German council wave had ~10,000 spontaneous councils but the KPD (founded weeks later, ~3,000 members) had zero seats on the Berlin executive (`.../dauve-authier/ch05.htm`, `.../sewell/chapter2.htm`). This argues against any formula that derives I_c purely from M_r; cadre-presence must be independently tracked and can be near-zero even when M_r is at its historical maximum.
6. **Tick granularity mismatch.** Several of the fastest-moving historical decisions (Lenin's Oct. 24 1917 letter: "this very evening, this very night," `archive/lenin/works/1917/oct/24.htm`) resolve on an hours timescale that a weekly tick cannot represent. If the design wants to capture "windows of opportunity that decay before the next tick," a sub-tick or event-flag mechanism may be needed in addition to the per-tick M_r/I_c update.
7. **Numeric coverage for growth-rate coefficients is thinner than for decay-rate coefficients.** History gives strong, cross-corroborated numbers for repression rollup/reconstitution timescales (Palmer Raids family of cases) but only two independent data points for the phase-change growth multiplier (Hunan, agricultural co-ops) and no clean per-tick number for baseline organic growth (AWO gives absolute scale, not a rate formula). Both growth-side coefficients above are flagged `NEEDS PLAYTEST` for that reason and should not be treated as equally well-grounded as the decay-side numbers.
