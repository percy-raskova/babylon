## Verdict

The sweep is broad (443 docs, low technical skip rate ~3%) and the seven briefs are well-sourced and self-aware about weak derivations, but it has three real classes of gaps. (1) The owner's specific numbered rulings are only partially closed: EH ruling 5 (decay re-derivation) is not actually answered — the briefs invent new M_r coefficients instead of reconciling fog-of-war.yaml's already-specified 0.20/0.05/0.01 I_c decay constants against the weekly tick; EH ruling 3 (per-org vs per-territory M_r scope) and DT rulings 3-5 (maintenance/decay, Study_Allocation time-fraction, Congress determinism mechanism) are touched tangentially but not resolved with a citable historical number or an explicit 'the corpus has no such case' admission. (2) Despite covering the four named scopes (US/Bolshevik/Chinese/general-revolutions/ProleWiki) at the surface level, several on-mirror, highly on-theme regions were never sampled at all: Indonesia/PKI 1965-66 (the single most extreme rollup/collapse case in the entire corpus, sitting unread in history/indonesia/), Spain/POUM (102 unused files, a textbook Desert-falsification-by-security-apparatus case), Yugoslavia/Greek Civil War (unused, directly relevant to Congress-determinism and liberated-zone-collapse questions), Paris Commune (canonical, unused), and Guevara/Debray foco primary sources (needed to resolve a tension three briefs raise but none investigate). (3) One coefficient (Minsaengdan's 250x purge ratio) is load-bearing across three briefs but rests on a single regime-authored memoir with no independent corroboration, and the ProleWiki export has a ~21% empty-stub rate that no brief flags as a corpus-wide caveat. Net: strong primary-source discipline where it looked, but the sweep under-covers extreme/edge cases that would most sharpen the decay and collapse coefficients, and leaves several of the owner's explicit numbered rulings formally open.
